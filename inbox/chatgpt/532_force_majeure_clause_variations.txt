### **Force Majeure Clause Variations Analysis**

#### **1. Common Language Across the Contracts**
The **Force Majeure** clauses across multiple contracts generally follow a standard format, allowing either party to be excused from obligations if an uncontrollable event occurs. These clauses include:
- **Acts of God** (e.g., natural disasters, severe weather events)
- **Government Authority Actions** (e.g., restrictions, shutdowns, emergency declarations)
- **Pandemic/Epidemic Events**
- **Terrorism, Riots, and War**
- **Strikes and Labor Disruptions** (except those caused by the Resort’s employees)

#### **2. Notable Variations in the Contracts**
Several documents contain Force Majeure provisions, with key differences in wording and obligations:

##### **A. Contract Model 002 Final & Anonymized Contract 002**
- Allows termination **without liability** if an event beyond a party’s control makes it "illegal or impossible" to perform.
- Specifically states that **attrition damages** will not apply if the group decides to proceed with the event but has lower attendance due to the force majeure event.
- **Deposits are refundable** if Force Majeure applies【29:0†Contract model 002 final.docx】【29:1†Anonymized_Contract002.docx】.

##### **B. Contract Model 003 Final**
- Expands the definition to include **domestic war and terrorism on domestic soil**, **government regulations**, and **pandemics within domestic borders**.
- Requires **written notice within 10 days** of the event occurring for a party to invoke Force Majeure.
- Includes an obligation that the party invoking Force Majeure must "promptly resume performance" once the situation ceases.
- Introduces a **curing provision**, where the Resort has **10 business days** to provide an equivalent alternative property to fulfill the agreement【29:7†Contract model 003 final.docx】.

##### **C. Contract Clause Library (Industry Best Practice)**
- Includes more **specific triggers** for termination, such as **civil disorder within 20 miles of the hotel** and **curtailment of transportation preventing 80% of attendees from arriving**.
- Allows for **partial cancellation** if a major reduction in attendance occurs.
- Requires a **refund of all deposits within 30 days** if Force Majeure applies【29:9†Contract Clause Library.docx】.

#### **3. Key Differences & Potential Risks**
| **Aspect** | **Contract Model 002 & Anonymized 002** | **Contract Model 003 Final** | **Industry Best Practice** |
|------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Definition of Force Majeure Events** | Broad but general | More detailed, includes domestic terrorism | Very detailed, includes travel curtailment |
| **Notice Requirement** | Not specified | Must notify the other party within 10 days | Must notify as soon as reasonably possible |
| **Attrition & Concessions** | Attrition is waived for FM-related cancellations | No mention of waived attrition | Allows reduction of room block |
| **Refund Policy** | Deposits are refunded | Deposits refunded only if FM applies | Refund within 30 days of notice |
| **Resumption of Performance** | Not mentioned | Requires parties to resume once FM event ceases | Not mentioned |

### **4. Recommendations**
- **Clarify Liability for Partial Performance**: If attendance is affected by Force Majeure, the clause should specify whether the group is still responsible for attrition fees.
- **Expand Scope to Include Travel Restrictions**: Industry best practices include **curtailment of transportation**, which is missing in some versions.
- **Set Clearer Deadlines for FM Notice & Refunds**: The **Contract Model 003 Final** version's **10-day notice** requirement is stricter than other contracts and should be explicitly agreed upon.
- **Consider a Partial Cancellation Option**: Some contracts allow for partial reductions (e.g., reducing the room block without penalty), which is beneficial in unpredictable scenarios.

### **Final Thoughts**
- **Contract Model 002 & Anonymized Contract 002** are **more favorable** to the client (full deposit refunds, no attrition penalties).
- **Contract Model 003 Final** is **more restrictive** (requires rebooking attempts, strict notice period).
- **Industry Best Practices** provide **the most flexibility** (considering partial cancellations and broader event definitions).

USER:
i did airtigh my masa madre and is full of water on the top, what should i do

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
If your **masa madre (sourdough starter)** has water on top, that’s actually quite common — it’s called **“hooch.”**  
It forms when the starter is **hungry** (hasn’t been fed in a while) or was **sealed too tightly** and built up internal pressure.

Here’s what to do, step by step:

---

### 🧪 Step 1. Identify the liquid
- **Color:**  
  - Clear or light brown = normal hooch  
  - Grayish or dark brown = still fine, just older  
  - Pink, orange, or moldy = discard everything, it’s contaminated

---

### 🧹 Step 2. Decide what to do with the hooch
- **Option 1 (for mild hooch):** Stir it back in. This gives a tangier flavor.
- **Option 2 (preferred if there’s a lot):** Pour it off before feeding. This helps rebalance hydration and acidity.

---

### 🍞 Step 3. Feed your starter
1. Discard about **half** of the starter.  
2. Add **equal parts flour and water by weight** (e.g., 100 g each).  
3. Mix until smooth and **loose, pancake-batter consistency**.  
4. Cover **loosely** (not airtight) — it needs airflow but protection from dust.

---

### ⚙️ Step 4. Storage & maintenance
- **Room temperature (daily use):** Feed every 24 h.  
- **Refrigerator (occasional use):** Feed once a week and let it bubble before chilling.  
- Never seal airtight — use a lid slightly ajar or a breathable cover.

---

If the smell is **pleasantly sour or yeasty**, your starter can recover quickly.  
If it smells **putrid or rotten**, start a new one.

once the masa madre is resdy how much should i use  for bread making

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
Excellent — once your **masa madre (sourdough starter)** is active and bubbly (doubles in size within 4–6 hours of feeding), here’s exactly how much to use depending on your recipe type:

---

### 🍞 **1. Standard Artisan Loaf (Basic Country Bread)**
- **Flour:** 500 g  
- **Water:** 350 g (70% hydration)  
- **Salt:** 10 g  
- **Active Starter:** **100 g (20% of flour weight)**  

➡️ This ratio (20%) gives you a balanced fermentation — good rise and mildly sour flavor.

---

### 🌿 **2. Mild Flavor, Slow Ferment (Less Tangy)**
- Use **50 g starter (10%)**  
  - Slower fermentation (8–12 hours bulk rise).  
  - Great for overnight proofs and subtle flavor.

---

### ⚡ **3. Strong Flavor, Fast Ferment (More Sour, Quicker Rise)**
- Use **150–200 g starter (30–40%)**  
  - Faster bulk rise (3–4 hours).  
  - More pronounced acidity and aroma.

---

### 🧮 **General Rule of Thumb**
Use **10%–30% of the total flour weight** as starter.

Formula:  
> Starter amount (g) = Flour weight (g) × 0.1–0.3

---

### 🕒 **Example Timing**
| Starter % | Room Temp Rise | Flavor |
|------------|----------------|--------|
| 10% | 10–12 hours | Mild |
| 20% | 6–8 hours | Balanced |
| 30–40% | 3–5 hours | Tangy / Sour |

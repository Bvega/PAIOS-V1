## 🎯 Steps to Plan Out the Video

1. **Define the vision**  
   - Mood / tone (happy, dark, nostalgic, dreamy, etc.)  
   - Story you want to tell (literal interpretation of lyrics vs. abstract visuals)  
   - Target audience & where you’ll release it (YouTube, IG Reels, TikTok, etc.)

2. **Break the song into sections**  
   - Intro, verses, chorus, bridge, outro  
   - Decide which parts need visual “highlights” (e.g. chorus vs. verse)

3. **Make a shot list**  
   - For each section, list what kind of visuals would match (scenery, people, abstract patterns, etc.)  
   - Think of pacing: cutting faster during chorus, slower during intros/outros

4. **Pick styles of clips on Storyblocks**  
   - Footage (real-life) vs. motion graphics / abstract video  
   - Color tone / lighting (bright, dark, warm tone, cool tone)  
   - Consistency: same visual style so video feels cohesive

5. **Download / license clips**  
   - Make sure licensing allows you to use them with music (Storyblocks has “industry-leading licensing” per their site) citeturn0view0  
   - Choose right resolution (1080p, 4K depending on output)

6. **Edit**  
   - Match visual cuts to musical cues (beat drops, tempo shifts)  
   - Use transitions / effects where they serve mood, not just decoration  
   - Maybe overlay lyrics or text if useful

7. **Final polish**  
   - Color grading to unify different clips  
   - Audio leveling (make sure visual transitions don’t interfere with vocal clarity)  
   - Add subtle motion graphics or overlays if needed (light leaks, dust, film grain, etc.)

---

## 🔍 What to Look for in Storyblocks

Here are types of stock clips or assets that often work well in music videos, and how to use them:

| Type | Why It Works | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Atmospheric landscapes / nature shots | Creates emotional, cinematic backdrop | For intros, instrumental breaks, slow parts |
| Slow-motion people / objects | Adds feeling, emphasis, movement without fast cuts | Chorus or emotional highs |
| Time-lapses / hyperlapses (cityscapes) | Suggests passage of time, energy, change | Builds into chorus / bridge |
| Macro / detail shots (hands, textures, water, lights) | Good for transitions, abstract emotion | Between main shots, to break monotony |
| Abstract motion backgrounds / textures | Good overlay / effect to add mood or color wash | Chorus, transitions, fade-ins / fade-outs |
| Motion graphics / templates (text, lyric intro) | Helps with lyric display, branding | Beginning, chorus with lyrics, end title |

---

## ✅ Tips to Maximize Impact

- **Sync cuts to musical moments** — if there’s a drum hit, snap to a visual cut; if music swells, let visuals swell too.  
- **Color & exposure matching** — different clip sources may vary; color grade so they look like they belong together.  
- **Keep visual interest** — avoid over-using the same kind of shot. Mix wide, medium, close.  
- **Don’t over-crowd** with effects; simple sometimes stronger.  
- **Consider audience attention span** — especially for short platforms, front-load interesting visuals.

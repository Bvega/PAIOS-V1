1. **Attrition Clause Enhancement**:
   - **Current Clause**: The group is allowed a 20% room reduction without penalty. However, if usage falls below 80%, the group must cover the difference.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: Consider adopting an attrition calculation based on "rooms actually used" rather than cumulative block. This would mitigate the group's liability if actual usage closely meets the contracted number, especially since the library includes clauses that allow shortfall to be measured against actual pickup.

2. **Cancellation Policy Modification**:
   - **Current Clause**: Stipulated percentages for cancellation (50%, 75%, and 100%) based on timing.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: Add language to clarify that cancellation charges are based on lost profit and include taxes. Additionally, a flexible rescheduling option could be beneficial. The library includes a clause where a cancellation fee may be reduced if the hotel is able to rebook a portion of the reserved space.

3. **Concessions Clause**:
   - **Current Clause**: Lists several concessions such as complimentary parking and internet access in guest and meeting rooms.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: Clarify that all concessions are contingent on meeting a minimum room block or food and beverage spend (e.g., "Complimentary parking subject to 90% room block pickup"). Also, specify the timeframe for concessions such as internet access, stating explicitly that these are for the event dates only.

4. **Relocation Clause**:
   - **Current Clause**: This clause may be missing or requires strengthening based on hotel policies.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: From the library, add a relocation clause that guarantees the hotel will provide accommodations at a nearby comparable property, along with transport, should it be unable to accommodate guests with confirmed reservations. Include compensation details, such as crediting rooms for commission and attrition calculations.

5. **Additional Spend/Hidden Fees**:
   - **Current Clause**: The contract doesn't explicitly restrict additional or hidden fees.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: Use a clause from the library to specify that any charges beyond those outlined in the contract require prior written approval from the group. This ensures no unanticipated fees are added, especially for amenities not listed.

6. **Force Majeure Clause**:
   - **Current Clause**: There is a brief force majeure section.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: Expand on this with language covering pandemics, government restrictions, and any situation rendering performance impracticable. This addition aligns with recent updates for comprehensive force majeure protections.

7. **Acceptance and Execution Timing**:
   - **Current Clause**: Includes an acceptance timeline, but it may not be firm.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: Specify a definitive execution deadline, after which the offer is void if not signed. This is particularly useful in cases where group scheduling or hotel room availability may change.

8. **Deposit and Payment**:
   - **Current Clause**: Payment is due seven days before the event.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: Ensure a phased deposit schedule is detailed, starting with a reservation deposit to secure the booking, followed by additional installments closer to the event. This would provide financial security for the hotel while allowing flexibility for the group.

9. **ADA Compliance Clause**:
   - **Current Clause**: It may not address ADA compliance comprehensively.
   - **Suggested Improvement**: Include an ADA clause requiring the hotel to meet accessibility needs for public areas, restrooms, and function rooms, with advance notification of any specific accommodations.

For the **Relocation Clause** (Point #4), I recommend adding a more comprehensive clause that offers protections to both the group and individual attendees if the hotel cannot accommodate guests with confirmed reservations. Here is an expanded relocation clause based on industry best practices and the library:

---

**Relocation Clause**  
In the unlikely event that the Hotel is unable to provide a guest room for a Group attendee with a confirmed reservation, the Hotel agrees to take the following steps to minimize inconvenience to the attendee and the Group:

1. **Alternative Accommodation**: The Hotel will arrange for accommodations at a comparable property within a reasonable distance. This alternative property must meet similar standards as the contracted Hotel and offer a comparable room rate to the rate agreed upon in this Contract.

2. **Transportation**: The Hotel will provide complimentary transportation to and from the alternative accommodation for the duration of the attendee’s relocation until their reservation can be honored at the Hotel.

3. **Priority for First Available Room**: Attendees relocated under this clause will be given priority for any available rooms at the Hotel. The Hotel will notify the relocated guest immediately upon the availability of a room and arrange for transportation back to the Hotel at no additional cost.

4. **Compensation**: In the event of relocation, any complimentary room credits, concessions, and other agreed-upon benefits in this Contract will continue to be credited toward the Group’s contracted room block and pickup requirements. This includes counting relocated room nights toward attrition and any other performance metrics.

5. **Notification and Approval**: Before relocating any attendee, the Hotel will notify the Group contact as early as possible to discuss the situation and receive consent for the relocation arrangements, ensuring that the Group is aware of all relocation details.

6. **Waiver of Charges**: Should any attendee decide to stay at the alternative accommodation for the entire event duration, the Hotel will waive any charges related to the original reservation and update the Group’s final pickup history to reflect these room nights as fully utilized.

7. **Acknowledgment of Responsibility**: A letter from the Hotel’s General Manager will be provided to relocated attendees, clearly stating that the relocation was due to an unexpected circumstance by the Hotel, not the Group’s responsibility. This letter will express the Hotel’s apologies and explain the relocation arrangements and associated benefits.

8. **Additional Credit for Major Inconveniences**: If relocation affects more than 5% of the Group’s total room block, the Hotel will provide an additional credit of 10% of the total anticipated room revenue, applied toward the Group’s final bill as a goodwill gesture.

---

**Rationale**:
This clause provides structured steps for handling unexpected room unavailability while safeguarding the Group’s contracted benefits, such as room count for attrition and performance credits. It also offers financial protections and transparency, ensuring the Group and attendees are informed, reimbursed, and prioritized for a quick return to the original accommodations. This approach strengthens the Group’s confidence in the Hotel’s commitment to guest satisfaction and compliance with the contractual agreement.

Here are some prominent academic journals focused on cognitive science:

1. **Cognitive Science**: This is a multidisciplinary peer-reviewed journal published by John Wiley & Sons on behalf of the Cognitive Science Society. Established in 1977, it covers all aspects of cognitive science, including knowledge representation, inference, memory processes, learning, problem solving, planning, perception, and natural language understanding【8†source】.

2. **Cognition**: An international journal that publishes theoretical and experimental papers on the study of the mind. Topics include behavior studies, language processing, memory, perception, and cognitive development【6†source】.

3. **Cognitive Psychology**: This journal focuses on important theoretical contributions in various areas of cognition such as memory, attention, perception, language processing, categorization, thinking, and reasoning. It also addresses the development and decline of cognitive processes over time【6†source】.

4. **Frontiers in Psychology - Cognitive Science**: Part of the largest journal in its field, this section explores all aspects of cognition from low-level sensory and motor processes to complex thinking and decision making【7†source】.

5. **Trends in Cognitive Sciences**: This journal provides comprehensive overviews of current thinking in cognitive sciences, aiming to keep scientists, students, and teachers updated on the latest developments in the field【6†source】.

These journals are essential for anyone researching cognitive science, providing access to the latest findings and theories in the field. For more detailed information or to access articles, you can visit their respective websites.

**Three Rivers Basmati Rice Cooking Instructions:**

**What is Basmati Rice?**
Long-grained, extremely aromatic, with a light earthy flavor, basmati is grown in Pakistan and India using traditional South Asian growing methods.

**Method of Cooking:**
1. **2 cups of Basmati rice**
2. **3 cups of boiling water**
3. **Pinch of salt (for taste)**

**Instructions:**
1. Rinse rice under cold water using a strainer.
2. Place rice within a large bowl filled with cold water, allowing the rice to submerge and leave for 30 minutes to 1 hour. This allows the rice to soften.
3. Drain rice and transfer it into a sauce pot with the addition of a pinch of salt once the rice is in the pot.
4. In a separate pot, boil 3 cups of water and then add it to the sauce pot containing the rice.
5. Place foil over the sauce pot, then cover with a lid. Apply medium-high heat to the pot to bring the water to steaming temperatures. Ensure the foil and lid are sealed completely so that steam cannot escape.
6. Once the water within the pot is boiling, turn the heat down to the lowest setting and allow the rice to cook for 15 minutes without disturbance.
7. After 15 minutes, turn off the heat completely and let the pot rest for 5 minutes without disruption.
8. Uncover the rice, and with a fork fluff all the rice and serve.
9. Enjoy your Three Rivers Nature's Longest Grain Basmati Rice!

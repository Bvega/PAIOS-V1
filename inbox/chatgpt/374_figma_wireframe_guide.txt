ASSISTANT:
1. **Create your desktop frame**  
   - In Figma’s toolbar, hit **F** (Frame tool) and draw a 1440×800 px frame (you can name it “Desktop – Simple Booking”).  

2. **Draw the left-purple half-circle**  
   1. Select the **Ellipse tool (O)** and draw a circle 1600×1600 px.  
   2. Place it so its rightmost edge aligns at x = 720 px (the midpoint of the frame).  
   3. With the circle selected, in the right panel under **Fill**, pick a purple gradient matching your reference.  
   4. Mask the left half: draw a 800×1600 px rectangle over the right half of the circle, select both, then **Right-click → Use as Mask**.  

3. **Import or build the phone mock-up**  
   - **Option A (import):** Drag in a standard iPhone frame SVG from the Figma Community.  
   - **Option B (build):**  
     1. Use **R** to draw a 250×520 px rounded-rectangle (32 px corner radius), fill white.  
     2. Add a top “notch” by drawing a small rectangle with matching corner radius, mask-subtract.  
   - Center this phone frame at x = 500 px, y = 400 px.

4. **Wireframe the chat header**  
   1. Inside the phone frame, draw a 250×60 px rectangle at the top, fill hot pink.  
   2. Place a 36 px-diameter circle on the left for the avatar; next to it, add two lines of placeholder text (“Samuel Green” and “Available to Walk”) using **T**.  
   3. Add a “<” icon on the left and a three-dot menu icon on the right.

5. **Layout chat bubbles**  
   - Using **R**, draw rounded rectangles (corner radius ~16 px) for messages:  
     - User messages: light gray fill, left-aligned.  
     - Walker messages: light purple fill, right-aligned.  
   - Stack them vertically with ~12 px of vertical spacing.

6. **Add the image thumbnails row**  
   - Draw three 50×50 px squares with 8 px corner radius side by side, fill light gray.  
   - Label below: a small text box.

7. **Create the booking options**  
   1. Draw two 220×48 px rounded rectangles (corner radius 24 px), fill gradient purple and pink respectively.  
   2. Inside each, place a small unfilled circle (radio button) on the left, text (“30 minute walk” / “1 hour walk”) in the middle, and price (“$29” / “$49”) right-aligned.  
   3. Space them with 16 px vertical gap.

8. **Wireframe the message input**  
   - At the bottom of the phone, draw a 250×40 px rounded rectangle, fill very light gray.  
   - Add placeholder text “Type a message…” and a circular “send” icon on the right.

9. **Add the heading and description**  
   1. On the right side of your desktop frame (starting at x = 780 px), use **T** to drop in a heading: “Simple booking” (H2, ~36 px, bold).  
   2. Below, add a paragraph block (~18 px, regular) with your descriptive text.  
   3. Align this text block vertically centered with the phone mock-up.

10. **Group & annotate**  
    - Select each major section (half-circle, phone, heading/text) and press **⌘G** to group.  
    - Rename layers/groups clearly (“BG-HalfCircle”, “Phone-Frame”, “Chat-Header”, “Bubble-User”, etc.) so your wireframe is organized and ready for prototyping.

You now have a clean, low-fidelity Figma wireframe matching the “Simple booking” layout.zoom in, adjust spacing, and you’re ready to hand off to your design or dev teams.

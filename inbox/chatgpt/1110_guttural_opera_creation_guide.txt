Creating an opera using only guttural sounds is an interesting and unconventional idea! An opera typically combines vocal and instrumental music with drama, and focusing on guttural sounds would create a unique, avant-garde experience. Here's a step-by-step approach to conceptualizing and creating such an opera:

### 1. **Define the Concept and Theme**

- **Theme:** Determine the central theme or storyline of the opera. Since there won't be traditional lyrics, the theme could be abstract, focusing on emotions, natural phenomena, or a mythological tale.
  
- **Characters:** Decide on the characters or entities involved. Characters can be personified elements, emotions, or mythical creatures.

### 2. **Structure the Opera**

- **Acts and Scenes:** Divide the opera into acts and scenes, each with a distinct purpose or mood. Consider having different sections that explore various themes or emotions.

- **Narrative Flow:** Even without words, the sounds should tell a story. Plan the progression of the narrative through the changes in tone, intensity, and texture of the sounds.

### 3. **Design the Soundscape**

- **Guttural Sounds:** Identify the types of guttural sounds you want to include, such as growls, grunts, throat singing, and vocal fry. Each sound can represent different characters or emotions.

- **Instrumental Accompaniment:** Decide if you want to include any musical instruments. Percussion instruments might complement guttural sounds well, enhancing rhythm and intensity.

- **Sound Dynamics:** Plan the dynamics of the performance, including volume, tempo, and pitch variations, to create a rich auditory experience.

### 4. **Compose the Music**

- **Score Writing:** Create a score that outlines the guttural sounds and any accompanying music. Use musical notation to indicate timing, intensity, and transitions.

- **Experimental Techniques:** Experiment with vocal techniques and combinations of sounds to achieve the desired effect.

### 5. **Choreograph the Performance**

- **Staging:** Design the staging, including the movement and interaction of performers. Guttural sounds can be complemented by expressive body language and movement.

- **Visual Elements:** Consider incorporating visual elements such as lighting, costumes, and set design to enhance the thematic elements and atmosphere.

### 6. **Rehearsal and Refinement**

- **Rehearsals:** Conduct rehearsals to refine the performance. Experiment with different combinations and sequences of sounds to achieve the desired impact.

- **Feedback and Adjustments:** Gather feedback from a small audience or collaborators to make necessary adjustments to the composition and performance.

### 7. **Performance and Recording**

- **Live Performance:** Stage the opera in a venue that supports experimental performances, such as an art gallery or avant-garde theater.

- **Recording:** Consider recording the performance to reach a wider audience. A video recording can capture both the auditory and visual elements.

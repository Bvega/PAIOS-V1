tranquilo, como las aguas de rio, que corre por su cauce. suave y en armonia como tu.

a travez de mi.

ASSISTANT:
To turn this evocative poem into a structured song following the Suno format (often used in Latin music styles), we can capture the deep yearning and the vivid imagery with distinct verses, a recurring chorus, and an emotional bridge. Here’s a suggested structure and some musical ideas to shape this into a song.

---

**Title:** *Parir un Poema*

### Structure:
**Verse 1**  
**Chorus**  
**Verse 2**  
**Chorus**  
**Bridge**  
**Verse 3**  
**Chorus**  
**Outro**

---

### Song Breakdown and Suggestions

---

#### **Verse 1**
> *"Necesito parir un poema fruto de nuestro amor,  
con dolores de no verte y no tenerte, con pujos de ansiedad."*

**Musical Mood:**  
Start softly, almost like a whisper, to express the vulnerability and longing. A slow tempo (around 70-80 BPM) with gentle guitar or piano could work well here, setting a heartfelt tone. The focus is on the tension of separation and yearning, so a simple, stripped-back melody could help convey the ache in these lines.

---

#### **Chorus**
> *"Necesito parir un poema que hable de ti, de lo que me das, de lo que me haces sentir.  
parir un poema digno de tu amor profundo y sincero,  
como tu alma blanca como copos de algodón."*

**Musical Mood:**  
This is the heart of the song, capturing the beauty of the love being described. The melody here could lift and expand, with more instrumentation joining in, like soft strings or layered harmonies to deepen the feeling. The chorus can repeat at the end of each verse to reinforce the central idea of giving birth to a poem as a testament to love.

---

#### **Verse 2**
> *"Necesito parir un poema, que describa tu hablar,  
que susurra como brisa, como eres,  
frágil y tierno como un niño."*

**Musical Mood:**  
The softness of this verse evokes a delicate melody, almost lullaby-like, to mirror the tenderness of the words. Here, you could add a gentle rhythm or light percussion to symbolize the pulse of love and the steadiness of affection, emphasizing the tender details about the loved one’s essence.

---

#### **Bridge**
> *"Necesito parir un poema, para que otros ojos y corazones puedan verte y conocerte,  
como me quieres, como fluye tu amor por mis venas, como torrente sanguíneo,  
en balance y sin presión."*

**Musical Mood:**  
The bridge serves as the emotional climax. It could be intensified musically with a shift to a fuller sound—perhaps by adding a powerful chord progression or vocal harmonies. This section captures the strength and flow of love, so think of a rhythm that feels like a heartbeat, steady but powerful, perhaps with bass or percussion coming in to support the melody’s ascent.

---

#### **Verse 3**
> *"Necesito parir un poema que diga, como llenas de besos mis días, besos sentidos que me calman y agitan."*

**Musical Mood:**  
Returning to a calmer, warm feel, this verse reflects a comforting rhythm, symbolizing the consistency of love in daily life. A slightly faster tempo here can convey the playful, joyous side of love. Think about soft percussion like hand drums or gentle strumming to bring energy into this verse, then slow it slightly as the song moves toward the final chorus.

---

#### **Outro**
> *"Necesito parir un poema que sea como tú, que hable de ti.  
que lleve tu nombre, un poema, que no sea,  
la entrega total de una soledad descarnada."*

**Musical Mood:**  
The outro could bring the song full circle, returning to the soft, intimate sound of the beginning, like a final reflection. A fading melody or single instrument could leave the listener with a sense of peace, almost like a whispered conclusion to echo the opening line. This outro leaves the song lingering in the air, emphasizing that the love continues beyond the words of the song.

---

### Additional Suggestions
- **Vocal Dynamics:** Consider a vocalist who can express the emotional highs and lows softly yet passionately. Vocal crescendos in the chorus will help emphasize the “birthing” of a poem as a release.
- **Instrumentation:** Acoustic guitar, soft piano, and light strings (such as violin or cello) would add warmth without overpowering the words. Percussion can be kept minimal and reserved for the more intense moments in the bridge.
- **Overall Tempo:** A moderate, flowing tempo between 70-80 BPM suits the balance between contemplation and expression of love. 

This structure and arrangement will help bring the poem’s raw emotion to life in song, giving it a pulse and movement that capture the intimacy and strength of the words.

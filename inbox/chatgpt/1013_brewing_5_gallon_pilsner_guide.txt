Brewing a 5-gallon batch of Pilsner-style beer is a great choice for a crisp, refreshing brew. Pilsners are known for their light malt flavor, clean finish, and moderate hop bitterness, making them a popular style for many beer lovers. Here’s a step-by-step guide to brewing a classic Pilsner:

### Ingredients

1. **Malt:**
   - 9 lbs (4 kg) Pilsner Malt

2. **Hops:**
   - 1 oz (28 g) Saaz Hops (60 minutes) - for bittering
   - 0.5 oz (14 g) Saaz Hops (30 minutes) - for flavor
   - 0.5 oz (14 g) Saaz Hops (10 minutes) - for aroma

3. **Yeast:**
   - Wyeast 2124 (Bohemian Lager) or White Labs WLP800 (Pilsner Lager) yeast

4. **Water:**
   - Soft water is ideal for Pilsner. If your tap water is hard, consider diluting with distilled water.

5. **Other:**
   - 1 Whirlfloc tablet (optional, for clarity)

### Equipment

- Brew kettle (at least 8 gallons capacity)
- Mash tun
- Fermentation vessel
- Wort chiller
- Hydrometer or refractometer
- Sanitizer (e.g., Star San)

### Brewing Process

#### Step 1: Mashing

1. **Heat Strike Water:**
   - Heat 3.5 gallons of water to around 164°F (73°C).

2. **Mash In:**
   - Add the crushed Pilsner malt to the mash tun. Pour the heated water over the grain and stir to eliminate clumps. Aim for a mash temperature of around 152°F (67°C).

3. **Mash Rest:**
   - Let the mash rest for 60 minutes, maintaining the temperature at 152°F (67°C).

4. **Mash Out:**
   - Heat the mash to 170°F (77°C) for 10 minutes to stop enzyme activity.

#### Step 2: Lautering and Sparging

1. **Lauter:**
   - Slowly drain the sweet wort from the mash tun into the brew kettle. Recirculate the first runnings until clear.

2. **Sparge:**
   - Sparge the grain bed with 170°F (77°C) water until you collect around 6.5 gallons of wort in your brew kettle.

#### Step 3: Boiling

1. **Boil the Wort:**
   - Bring the wort to a rolling boil. Boil for 60 minutes.

2. **Hop Additions:**
   - Add 1 oz (28 g) of Saaz hops at the beginning of the boil (60 minutes).
   - Add 0.5 oz (14 g) of Saaz hops with 30 minutes left in the boil.
   - Add 0.5 oz (14 g) of Saaz hops with 10 minutes left in the boil.
   - Optionally, add a Whirlfloc tablet with 15 minutes left to help clarify the beer.

#### Step 4: Cooling and Fermentation

1. **Cool the Wort:**
   - Use a wort chiller to cool the wort rapidly to around 50°F (10°C).

2. **Transfer and Pitch Yeast:**
   - Transfer the cooled wort to a sanitized fermenter. Aerate the wort by shaking or using an aeration stone. Pitch the lager yeast into the fermenter.

3. **Fermentation:**
   - Ferment at 50°F (10°C) for 2-3 weeks. After primary fermentation is complete, raise the temperature to 65°F (18°C) for a diacetyl rest for 2-3 days.

#### Step 5: Lagering and Packaging

1. **Lagering:**
   - After the diacetyl rest, slowly lower the temperature to 35°F (2°C) over several days. Lager for 4-6 weeks.

2. **Packaging:**
   - Once lagering is complete, bottle or keg the beer. If bottling, prime with 4 oz of corn sugar. If kegging, carbonate to around 2.5 volumes of CO2.

### Additional Tips

- **Water Profile:** Aim for a soft water profile. Use distilled water or reverse osmosis (RO) water and adjust minerals if necessary.
- **Temperature Control:** Keep a close eye on fermentation temperatures, as Pilsners require a consistent and cool fermentation.

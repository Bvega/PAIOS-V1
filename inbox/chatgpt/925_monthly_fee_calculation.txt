USER:
based on a 1,200 ASSOCIATES IN A COMPANY, what would be the monthly fee on a 3 year basis to recover a 700,00 investment

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
To calculate the monthly fee that would recover a $700,000 investment over 3 years, assuming there are 1,200 associates, the steps are as follows:

1. **Determine the total number of months in 3 years:**
   - 3 years = 36 months.

2. **Calculate the total monthly fee required to recover $700,000 over 36 months:**
   \[
   \text{Monthly fee required} = \frac{\text{Investment}}{\text{Number of months}} = \frac{700,000}{36} = 19,444.44
   \]

3. **Determine the fee per associate:**
   \[
   \text{Fee per associate} = \frac{\text{Monthly fee required}}{\text{Number of associates}} = \frac{19,444.44}{1,200} = 16.20
   \]

So, the monthly fee per associate to recover the $700,000 investment over 3 years would be approximately **$16.20**.

ASSISTANT:
To calculate the Return on Investment (ROI) with a time gap (e.g., considering a delayed start in cash inflows or uneven cash flows), we need to take into account the following:

1. **Investment Amount (Initial Cost):** $700,000
2. **Revenue per Month:** \$16.20 (monthly fee per associate) × 1,200 associates = \$19,440 per month
3. **Time Period:** 36 months (3 years)
4. **Delayed Start in Revenue (if any):** You need to specify this time gap or delay.

Let's assume there is a **6-month delay** in generating revenue, meaning that the company will not receive any income for the first 6 months but will receive the same monthly fee of \$19,440 after the delay.

### Steps for ROI Calculation:

1. **Determine total revenue over 3 years minus the delay:**

   After the 6-month delay, you have **30 months** of revenue generation.

   \[
   \text{Total revenue after delay} = 19,440 \times 30 = 583,200
   \]

2. **ROI Calculation:**

   ROI is typically calculated as:

   \[
   \text{ROI} = \frac{\text{Net Profit}}{\text{Initial Investment}} \times 100
   \]

   Here, Net Profit = Total Revenue - Initial Investment

   \[
   \text{Net Profit} = 583,200 - 700,000 = -116,800
   \]

   The ROI would be negative in this case due to the shortfall in revenue:

   \[
   \text{ROI} = \frac{-116,800}{700,000} \times 100 = -16.69\%
   \]

   **Conclusion:** With a 6-month delay in revenue generation, the ROI after 3 years would be **-16.69%**, indicating that the investment would not be fully recovered within that time frame.

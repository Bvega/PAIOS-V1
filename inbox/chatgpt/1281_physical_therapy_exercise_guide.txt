### 1. Supine Shoulder Flexion AROM
**Script:**
"In our first exercise, Supine Shoulder Flexion, start by lying flat on your back on a stable surface. Place your arms at your sides with your palms facing the ceiling. Slowly raise your arms above your head, keeping your arms straight. This movement should stretch your shoulders and arms upward as far as you can comfortably go. Hold for a moment, then gently lower back to the starting position. We'll do this for three sets of 15 repetitions, with a rest between sets."

### 2. Side Lying Shoulder External Rotation with Dumbbell
**Script:**
"For the Side Lying Shoulder External Rotation, lie on your side on a flat bench. Hold a dumbbell in the upper hand with your arm bent at a 90-degree angle, and your forearm perpendicular to your body. Keep your elbow pinned to your side. Slowly rotate your forearm upwards until it is parallel to the ground, then bring it back down. Perform three sets of 10 repetitions on each side. Take a short break between sets."

### 3. Prone Unilateral Shoulder Flexion
**Script:**
"Next, we move to Prone Unilateral Shoulder Flexion. Lie face down on a bench, letting one arm hang freely to the side of the bench. With a dumbbell in hand, slowly lift your arm straight ahead until it is level with your body, then lower it back down gently. Ensure your movements are controlled, focusing on engaging your shoulder muscles. We will do three sets of 10 repetitions for each arm."

### 4. Prone Unilateral T
**Script:**
"Our final exercise is the Prone Unilateral T. Remain prone on the bench, but this time extend your arm to the side, forming a 'T' with your body. Lift the arm upward, keeping it straight and aligned with your shoulder, then gently lower it back down. This exercise will help strengthen your shoulder blade muscles. Perform three sets of 10 repetitions on each side."

### Conclusion
**Script:**
"Great job completing the session! Remember, consistency is key to recovery and improvement. Repeat these exercises as recommended by your therapist, and always listen to your body to avoid overexertion. Thank you for joining me today, and I look forward to our next session together.

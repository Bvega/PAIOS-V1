ASSISTANT:
**1. Key difference between authentication and authorization + analogy:**  
- **Authentication** = proving **who you are**.  
- **Authorization** = deciding **what you’re allowed to do**.  
- **Analogy**: At an airport, showing your **ID at security** is authentication. Using your **boarding pass to enter your gate** is authorization.

---

**2. Why it's important to differentiate between session-based and token-based authentication:**  
They handle login and access differently:  
- **Session-based** stores login info on the server (good for traditional websites).  
- **Token-based** sends a token with each request (better for APIs and mobile apps).  
Mixing them up can lead to bad performance, security issues, or broken login features.

---

**3. Risks if authentication is not implemented properly:**  
- **Hackers can log in as someone else** (identity theft)  
- **Private data can be stolen**  
- **Users can access things they shouldn’t**  
- **Accounts can be hijacked or taken over**  

Proper setup protects users and keeps your system safe.

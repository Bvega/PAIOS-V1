OG Hops, or *Humulus lupulus*, is primarily used in brewing beer for its bittering, flavoring, and aromatic qualities. However, it also has several traditional and medicinal uses in herbal medicine:

1. **Sedative and Sleep Aid**: OG Hops is commonly used to help with insomnia and anxiety due to its natural sedative effects. It is often combined with valerian root in herbal preparations for enhanced sleep support.

2. **Digestive Support**: Hops may stimulate appetite and aid digestion. It has been traditionally used to relieve indigestion and bloating.

3. **Menopausal Symptom Relief**: The phytoestrogens in hops are believed to help balance hormones and may relieve symptoms like hot flashes.

4. **Anti-inflammatory and Antimicrobial**: Hops contain compounds that may reduce inflammation and exhibit mild antimicrobial properties.

5. **Mood and Stress Support**: It is sometimes used in herbal formulations to calm the nervous system and manage stress.

These benefits are largely based on traditional use and preliminary studies. For clinical effectiveness, more research is necessary. Always consult a healthcare provider before using hops medicinally, especially if pregnant, breastfeeding, or on medication.

How do you use it?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
OG Hops can be used in several ways depending on the intended benefit. Here are common methods:

1. **Tea/Infusion**:
   - Steep 1–2 teaspoons of dried hop cones in hot water for 10–15 minutes.
   - Drink before bed to promote relaxation and improve sleep.

2. **Tincture**:
   - Hops tincture (alcohol-based extract) is taken in small doses (typically 0.5–1 mL).
   - Often used for stress, anxiety, or insomnia. Follow label or practitioner guidance.

3. **Capsules/Tablets**:
   - Available as supplements, often combined with other calming herbs like valerian or passionflower.
   - Used for sleep or menopausal support.

4. **Aromatherapy/Pillow Sachets**:
   - Dried hops placed in a sachet or pillow can be used near the head during sleep.
   - The scent is believed to help induce drowsiness.

5. **Topical Use**:
   - Infused in oils or salves for mild anti-inflammatory effects on the skin.

6. **Baths**:
   - A handful of dried hops can be added to a muslin bag or directly into warm bath water for relaxation.

**Important Tips**:
- Start with small doses to see how your body reacts.
- Use consistently for best effects on sleep and stress.
- Consult a healthcare provider if you're on medication or have health conditions, especially hormonal or psychiatric disorders.

**Red clover blossom** (*Trifolium pratense*) is a popular herb in traditional and modern herbal medicine, especially valued for its phytoestrogens. Here's a summary of its uses and benefits:

### Medicinal Uses and Benefits

1. **Menopausal Symptom Relief**:
   - Red clover is rich in isoflavones, plant compounds that mimic estrogen. These may help reduce hot flashes, night sweats, and other menopause symptoms.

2. **Hormonal Balance**:
   - Often used for PMS and menstrual irregularities due to its mild estrogen-like effects.

3. **Cardiovascular Support**:
   - Isoflavones in red clover may support heart health by improving arterial flexibility and reducing LDL cholesterol.

4. **Skin Health**:
   - Used externally for eczema, psoriasis, and other skin irritations due to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties.

5. **Bone Health**:
   - Some studies suggest red clover may help maintain bone density in postmenopausal women, though evidence is still emerging.

6. **Detoxification**:
   - Traditionally used as a blood purifier and lymphatic cleanser.

### How to Use

- **Tea/Infusion**: Steep 1–2 teaspoons of dried blossoms in hot water for 10–15 minutes. Drink 1–3 cups daily.
- **Tincture**: Typically 1–2 mL up to three times daily, following label or practitioner instructions.
- **Capsules/Tablets**: Standardized isoflavone extracts are available, often used for menopause support.
- **Topical Use**: Infused in creams or salves for skin issues.
- **Poultice**: Crushed blossoms can be applied to skin for minor wounds or irritations.

### Safety Notes

- **Avoid during pregnancy and breastfeeding** without professional advice.
- **Use cautiously if you have hormone-sensitive conditions** like breast or uterine cancer.
- May interact with blood thinners due to its coumarin content.

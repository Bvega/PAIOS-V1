The famous meridian that divides the United States into two halves (east and west) is the **100th meridian west**.

It runs north-south and is often considered a symbolic dividing line between the humid eastern U.S. and the arid western plains. Historically, it has marked a major environmental and agricultural boundary, especially noted in geography and climate studies.

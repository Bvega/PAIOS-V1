ASSISTANT:
It sounds like your fresh apple juice is already showing some signs of wild fermentation (the foam and residues could be from natural yeast starting to work).  
We can harness that or start fresh with controlled fermentation for a clean, tasty apple cider.  

Here’s a **step-by-step process** for turning your 1 gallon of fresh juice into cider:  

---

## **1. Decide on Wild vs. Controlled Fermentation**
- **Wild fermentation**: Use the yeast already in your juice. This gives more complex, funky flavors but can be unpredictable.
- **Controlled fermentation**: Kill off wild yeast and pitch your own cider yeast (like Lalvin EC-1118 or SafCider). This gives a cleaner, more predictable result.

Since you already have foam, you might:
- **Either** embrace the wild ferment (skip sanitizing and just keep fermenting)
- **Or** pasteurize/sulfite the juice and add your own yeast.

---

## **2. Sanitize Everything**
Regardless of yeast choice, clean and sanitize:
- Fermenter (1-gallon glass jug or food-grade bucket)
- Airlock and bung
- Funnel, spoon, hydrometer (if you have one)

**Why?** To avoid mold, vinegar bacteria, or unwanted off-flavors.

---

## **3. (Optional) Prepare the Juice**
If doing controlled fermentation:
- Heat juice to **160°F (71°C) for 10 minutes** OR  
- Add **1 Campden tablet** per gallon and wait 24 hours before adding yeast.

---

## **4. Pitch the Yeast**
- If going wild: skip this — nature is already at work.
- If controlled: Rehydrate yeast according to package directions, then add to juice.

---

## **5. Add Fermentation Lock**
- Pour juice into fermenter, leaving at least 2 inches of headspace.
- Fit airlock and bung.  
- Keep at **60–70°F (15–21°C)** in a dark place.

---

## **6. Primary Fermentation**
- Duration: **1–3 weeks** until bubbling slows or stops.
- Signs it’s ready: No visible foam, no more airlock activity, and sediment (lees) at the bottom.

---

## **7. Racking**
- Carefully siphon cider off sediment into a clean container.
- You can taste it now — it will be dry unless you stop fermentation early.

---

## **8. Secondary Fermentation / Aging**
- Age in a clean jug for **1–3 months** for smoother flavor.
- You can rack again if more sediment forms.

---

## **9. Bottling**
- For still cider: Bottle as-is, no sugar added.
- For sparkling cider: Add **1 tsp priming sugar per 12 oz** bottle before capping.

---

## **10. Conditioning**
- Still cider: Ready immediately after bottling.
- Sparkling cider: Store at room temp 2–3 weeks, then chill and enjoy.

---

💡 **Tip:** Your foam right now suggests it’s already fermenting. If you like adventurous flavors, you could simply rack it into a sanitized jug with an airlock and let it finish naturally — you’d have a rustic farmhouse cider in a few weeks.

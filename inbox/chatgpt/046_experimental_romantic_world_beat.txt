A **nylon-string guitar** carries expressive counter-rhythms (harmonics, sparse rasgueos, muted flicks), occasionally “prepared” with felt/paper for soft percussive timbre.    - `#Pre – feel 7/8, keep lines short`

# UX in practice
- **Simple mode:** choose one preset, pick meter, click **Copy** → paste into Suno.  
- **Combined mode:** select two presets to fuse; optional **Meter-Flip** and **Alt-Mix** toggles; click **Copy**.  
- **Lyric-guided mode:** paste lyrics → the app suggests **role-splits** and **meter-trim**; you accept or tweak → click **Copy**.  
- **Log winners:** one click to save your final Prompt/Style/Lyrics to a local “vault” for reuse.

# Why this approach is optimal
- It aligns with **how Suno actually listens**: the Prompt + Style fields set broad behavior, while the **Lyrics box can meaningfully steer delivery/phrasing** in v4.5. citeturn0search2  
- It sidesteps API uncertainty and **ToS risks** from unofficial endpoints. citeturn1search14turn1search4  
- It’s fast to ship and evolves easily as Suno **changes models** (e.g., v4.5, model deprecations). citeturn1search0turn1search13

# Technical outline (non-executing)
- **Frontend:** React/TypeScript single-page app.

# Risks & mitigations
- **Time-signature isn’t “hard-locked”.** Suno responds to hints; sometimes it drifts toward 4/4. Mitigate by embedding meter language in **both Prompt and Lyrics**, and by using the **Song Editor** to nudge sections after generation. citeturn0search13  
- **Voice guidance is probabilistic.** Keep cues simple, repeated at section starts. v4.5 reads lyric-box context better than older models. citeturn0search2  
- **Ecosystem churn.** Keep presets model-agnostic and versioned; update language if Suno’s model guidance shifts (e.g., future v5).

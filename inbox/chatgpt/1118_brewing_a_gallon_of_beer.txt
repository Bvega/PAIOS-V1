Great choice! An India Pale Ale (IPA) is a popular and flavorful style known for its strong hop character and bitterness. Making a one-gallon batch of IPA at home is a fun project, and I can guide you through the process.

### Ingredients

Here are the ingredients you'll need for a one-gallon batch of IPA:

1. **Malt Extract**:
   - **Dry Malt Extract (DME)**: 1.25 lbs (light or pale)
   - Alternatively, you can use 1.5 lbs of liquid malt extract (LME)

2. **Specialty Grains** (optional but adds complexity):
   - **Crystal Malt**: 0.1 lbs (40L for some sweetness and color)

3. **Hops**:
   - **Bittering Hops**: 0.3 oz (e.g., Cascade, Centennial, or Chinook)
   - **Flavor Hops**: 0.2 oz (e.g., Citra or Amarillo)
   - **Aroma Hops**: 0.2 oz (e.g., Simcoe or Mosaic)
   - Optionally, you can add dry hops in secondary fermentation for extra aroma: 0.2 oz (e.g., Cascade or Citra)

4. **Yeast**:
   - **Ale Yeast**: 1 packet (e.g., Safale US-05 or Wyeast 1056)

5. **Water**:
   - 1.5 gallons of water (you’ll lose some during the boil and fermentation)

6. **Priming Sugar** (for bottling):
   - 0.75 oz corn sugar (for carbonation)

### Equipment

- **1-gallon glass carboy or fermenter**
- **Airlock and stopper**
- **Brewing kettle (at least 2-gallon capacity)**
- **Strainer or muslin bag for grains**
- **Thermometer**
- **Hydrometer (optional, for measuring specific gravity)**
- **Sanitizer** (e.g., Star San or Iodophor)
- **Bottles and caps** (approximately 10-12 bottles)

### Brewing Process

#### 1. Preparation

- **Sanitize**: Clean and sanitize all equipment that will come in contact with your beer to avoid contamination.

#### 2. Steep Specialty Grains (optional)

- **Heat Water**: Heat 1 gallon of water in your kettle to about 150-160°F (65-71°C).
- **Steep Grains**: Add the crushed specialty grains to a muslin bag and steep in the heated water for about 20 minutes.
- **Remove Grains**: Remove the grains and let them drain into the kettle. Do not squeeze the bag to avoid extracting tannins.

#### 3. Boil

- **Bring to a Boil**: Bring the water to a boil.
- **Add Malt Extract**: Remove the kettle from heat and stir in the malt extract until fully dissolved. Return to heat and bring to a boil again.
- **Add Bittering Hops**: Add your bittering hops at the start of the boil (60 minutes).
- **Add Flavor Hops**: Add flavor hops with 15 minutes remaining in the boil.
- **Add Aroma Hops**: Add aroma hops with 5 minutes remaining in the boil.

#### 4. Cooling

- **Cool the Wort**: After boiling, cool the wort quickly by placing the kettle in an ice bath. Cool to around 70°F (21°C).

#### 5. Fermentation

- **Transfer to Fermenter**: Pour the cooled wort into the sanitized fermenter, leaving behind the sediment (trub).
- **Top Up**: Add enough water to bring the total volume to 1 gallon.
- **Aerate**: Shake the fermenter to aerate the wort, which helps the yeast.
- **Pitch Yeast**: Sprinkle or pour the yeast into the fermenter.
- **Seal**: Attach the airlock and stopper.

#### 6. Ferment

- **Primary Fermentation**: Place the fermenter in a cool, dark place. Ferment at about 65-72°F (18-22°C) for 1-2 weeks.
- **Optional Secondary Fermentation**: Transfer to a secondary fermenter for another week if you want to add dry hops.

#### 7. Bottling

- **Prepare Priming Sugar**: Dissolve 0.75 oz of corn sugar in a small amount of boiling water. Cool and add to the bottling bucket.
- **Transfer Beer**: Siphon the beer into the bottling bucket, mixing with the priming sugar solution.
- **Bottle**: Fill sanitized bottles and cap them.

#### 8. Carbonation

- **Condition**: Store the bottles at room temperature for 1-2 weeks to carbonate.

#### 9. Enjoy

- **Chill and Enjoy**: Refrigerate your bottles, pour into a glass, and enjoy your homemade IPA!

### Tips for Success

- **Sanitization**: Keep everything sanitized to prevent infections.
- **Temperature Control**: Maintain a stable fermentation temperature.
- **Patience**: Allow your beer to fully ferment and condition.

If you have any questions or need clarification on any step, feel free to ask!

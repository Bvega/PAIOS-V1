Yes, there are a few APIs you can use for a **tea website**, depending on your goals. Here are some useful ones categorized by purpose:

---

### 🫖 **Tea-Specific APIs**
There are *very few dedicated tea APIs*, but you can use **general food and ingredient APIs** or create your own small dataset.

#### 1. **[TheTeaAPI (unofficial)]**
- *Status*: Community-maintained / may need local hosting
- *Data*: Tea types, brewing methods, caffeine content, descriptions
- *URL*: Often shared on GitHub, not officially hosted. Let me know if you want a local version built.

---

### 🍵 **Alternative APIs You Can Use**

#### 2. **Spoonacular API**
- *Type*: Comprehensive food and recipe database
- *Use for*: Tea recipes, ingredients, health benefits
- *Features*:
  - Nutritional info
  - Recipe steps
  - Food trivia / tea facts
- *Link*: https://spoonacular.com/food-api

#### 3. **Edamam Food Database API**
- *Use for*: Ingredient info (e.g., types of tea, health effects, caffeine content)
- *Features*: Nutrition, allergens, health labels
- *Link*: https://developer.edamam.com/

#### 4. **Open Food Facts API**
- *Use for*: Searching tea brands and products
- *Link*: https://world.openfoodfacts.org/data

#### 5. **Nutritionix API**
- *Use for*: U.S. based food items, including commercial teas
- *Link*: https://www.nutritionix.com/business/api

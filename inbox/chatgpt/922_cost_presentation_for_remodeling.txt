ASSISTANT:
To professionally present all costs for your apartment remodeling project to your client, I recommend creating a clear and organized cost breakdown in a well-structured report. Here’s how you can do it:

### 1. **Use a Professional Template**
   - Create the document in a program like Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets, or a professional accounting software that can generate clear reports.
   - Alternatively, a PDF or Word document can be used with well-structured tables.

### 2. **Organize the Costs by Category**
   Break down the costs into key categories:
   - **Materials**: List all the materials used, quantity, unit price, and total cost.
   - **Labor**: Detail the labor costs, including hourly rates or project-based fees.
   - **Subcontractors**: Include fees for any external professionals like electricians, plumbers, etc.
   - **Permits and Inspections**: Include any legal fees or costs associated with permits.
   - **Miscellaneous**: Any other costs like transportation, equipment rental, etc.

### 3. **Include a Project Summary**
   - Provide an overview of the project, including the start and completion date, scope of the work, and total cost.
   - You can add a comparison between the initial budget and the final expenses to highlight cost efficiency.

### 4. **Visual Elements**
   - Use charts or graphs to show cost breakdowns (e.g., pie charts or bar graphs) to make the data easier to digest visually.
   - Include photos of before and after if relevant to showcase value.

### 5. **Breakdown by Phase or Room**
   If the project involved different phases (e.g., design, demolition, construction) or areas (e.g., kitchen, living room, bathroom), you can present costs per phase or room.

### 6. **Provide a Payment Summary**
   - Summarize all payments received and the balance due, if any.
   - Make it easy for your client to understand what's been paid and what remains outstanding.

### 7. **Include Terms and Conditions**
   - If applicable, include any payment terms, warranties, or follow-up services related to the remodeling.

Once you’ve compiled everything, export it as a clean PDF for professional presentation or share it digitally if needed.

The price estimates I provided typically **do not** include project management fees. Project management is often an additional cost, especially for complex remodels like a full apartment renovation.

### **Project Management Costs**
- Project management fees usually range from **10% to 20%** of the total project cost, depending on the complexity and the level of involvement required.
- For a project with a total cost of $98,000 to $204,000, you can estimate an additional:
   - **Low end**: $9,800 - $19,600
   - **High end**: $20,400 - $40,800

This covers the project manager's responsibilities, such as:
- Coordinating contractors and subcontractors
- Overseeing daily site operations
- Ensuring the project stays on schedule and within budget
- Managing materials, permits, and inspections

If you include project management in the total budget, your overall costs would increase accordingly.

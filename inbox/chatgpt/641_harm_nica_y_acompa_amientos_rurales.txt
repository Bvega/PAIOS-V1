   - Often paired with the harmonica in folk and country music.
   - Provides rhythmic and harmonic support to the harmonica’s melodies.

2. **Banjo**  
   - Adds a bright, plucky sound that complements the harmonica in bluegrass and folk genres.

3. **Fiddle (Violin)**  
   - A common accompaniment in country or Appalachian music, adding lyrical or rhythmic ornamentation.

4. **Dobro (Resonator Guitar)**  
   - Enhances the warm, resonant tones often associated with Americana and blues.

5. **Mandolin**  
   - Contributes a delicate, chiming quality that blends well with the harmonica in rustic or folk contexts.

6. **Accordion**  
   - Shares the harmonica’s free-reed mechanism, creating a fuller sound in folk and world music genres.

7. **Bass (Double Bass or Upright Bass)**  
   - Grounds the ensemble with a deep, steady rhythm.

8. **Percussion (Hand Drums or Washboard)**  
   - Provides texture and rhythmic drive, often in folk or blues settings.

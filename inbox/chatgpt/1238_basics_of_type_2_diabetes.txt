Type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) is a chronic condition that affects the way your body metabolizes glucose (sugar). Here are the basics:

### Understanding Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus:

1. **Insulin Resistance**:
   - In T2DM, the body's cells become resistant to the effects of insulin, a hormone produced by the pancreas that regulates blood sugar.
   - The pancreas tries to compensate by producing more insulin, but eventually, it can't keep up, leading to high blood sugar levels.

2. **Causes and Risk Factors**:
   - **Genetic Factors**: Family history of diabetes can increase risk.
   - **Lifestyle Factors**: Obesity, poor diet, physical inactivity, and sedentary lifestyle.
   - **Age**: Risk increases with age, particularly after age 45.
   - **Other Factors**: High blood pressure, high cholesterol, and polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

3. **Symptoms**:
   - Increased thirst and hunger.
   - Frequent urination.
   - Fatigue.
   - Blurred vision.
   - Slow-healing sores or frequent infections.
   - Unexplained weight loss.

4. **Diagnosis**:
   - **HbA1c Test**: Measures average blood glucose over the past 2-3 months. A level of 6.5% or higher indicates diabetes.
   - **Fasting Blood Sugar Test**: A level of 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L) or higher after an overnight fast.
   - **Oral Glucose Tolerance Test**: A level of 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) or higher two hours after drinking a glucose-containing beverage.

5. **Management and Treatment**:
   - **Lifestyle Changes**: Healthy diet (low in refined sugars and carbohydrates, high in fiber), regular physical activity, and weight loss.
   - **Medications**: Metformin is commonly prescribed to lower blood sugar levels. Other medications include sulfonylureas, meglitinides, and insulin therapy if needed.
   - **Monitoring**: Regular blood sugar monitoring to manage levels and prevent complications.

6. **Complications**:
   - Heart disease and stroke.
   - Neuropathy (nerve damage), particularly in the legs.
   - Nephropathy (kidney damage).
   - Retinopathy (eye damage) and potential blindness.
   - Foot damage leading to infections and potentially amputations.
   - Skin conditions and increased risk of infections.

### Prevention:
- **Maintain a Healthy Weight**: Aim for a balanced diet and regular physical activity.
- **Regular Exercise**: At least 30 minutes of moderate activity most days.
- **Healthy Eating**: Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit sugar and refined carbs.
- **Regular Check-ups**: Monitor blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and blood sugar levels, especially if you have risk factors for diabetes.

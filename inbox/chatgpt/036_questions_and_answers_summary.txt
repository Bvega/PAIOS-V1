# **IBM Open e-Learning Ecosystem – Reflection: Explainability in AI**

### **Lesson: Making AI Systems More Explainable**

---

### **Question 1**  
**What is a way for the team to be aware of who is involved in development and deployment of the AI system and each of their roles?**

**Filled Answer:**  
The team should start by clearly defining all roles and responsibilities before any technical development begins—right after the initial business discussion stage. A responsibility matrix or stakeholder map can document who is involved in each phase of the project, including data collection, modeling, validation, deployment, and monitoring.  
To build trust and accountability, the team should ensure representation from a diverse and inclusive group of participants, covering technical experts, ethical reviewers, user-experience designers, and business stakeholders. Maintaining this diversity promotes balanced perspectives and supports the creation of fair and trustworthy AI systems.  

---

### **Question 2**  
**How can the team gather the type of explanations each persona will aim to get from the model?**

**Filled Answer:**  
The process begins with defining the key personas that interact with or evaluate the AI system—such as Users, Approvers/Auditors, and Data Scientists/Validators.  
The team should conduct workshops or interviews with representatives of each persona to identify what they need to understand about the model. For example, Users may want to know why a recommendation was made, while Auditors may want insight into the model’s decision path for compliance checks.  
Once these needs are identified, the team can co-design communication formats (dashboards, reports, tooltips, or explainable-AI visualizations) that deliver the right level of information without complicating or interrupting the user experience.  

---

### **Question 3**  
**Which explanation method will better match with each persona’s expectation?**

**Filled Answer:**  
Each persona requires a different type of explanation method:  

- **Users (Embracing Discovery):** Prefer intuitive explanations showing *why* specific recommendations appear and *how* their actions can influence future outcomes. Techniques like example-based explanations or simple feature highlights are effective.  
- **Approvers & Auditors:** Need transparent documentation of model logic and feature influence for auditing and compliance. Methods such as decision-path visualizations, rule extractions, and traceable logs are ideal.  
- **Data Scientists & Model Validators:** Seek quantitative evaluation of feature impact, model accuracy, and overall performance. They rely on statistical or visualization-based explainability tools like SHAP, LIME, or feature-importance graphs.  

Selecting explanation methods that align with each persona’s goals ensures that explanations are meaningful and usable. Applying **design-thinking principles** helps match communication style and depth to each group’s understanding level.  

---

### **Summary Insight**
Explainability is not a single output—it is an ecosystem practice involving clear role definition, persona-specific information gathering, and tailored communication. Combining **diversity, collaboration, and design thinking** helps create transparent and accountable AI systems that everyone can understand and trust.

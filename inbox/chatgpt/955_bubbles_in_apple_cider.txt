USER:
I am making apple cider , my batch has already two weeks but I stille see bubbles in my bottle, what does that means?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
Seeing bubbles in your cider after two weeks indicates that fermentation is still actively occurring. The bubbles are carbon dioxide being released as yeast continues to consume the sugars in the apple juice, converting them into alcohol and CO2.

Here's what you can do:

1. **Check the Specific Gravity**: Use a hydrometer to measure the specific gravity of your cider. This will help determine if fermentation is complete. The reading should be stable over a few days (typically below 1.010 for most ciders when fermentation is finished).

2. **Wait Longer**: Fermentation times can vary, especially depending on temperature, yeast strain, and initial sugar content. It’s not uncommon for cider to ferment for several weeks.

3. **Monitor Airlock Activity**: If you’re using an airlock, monitor the bubbling rate. As fermentation nears completion, the bubbles should slow down significantly.

4. **Temperature Check**: Ensure your fermentation temperature is within the optimal range for your yeast strain (usually 60-75°F / 15-24°C). If it's too cold, fermentation can take longer.

5. **Patience is Key**: It's best not to bottle until fermentation is complete, as bottling too early can lead to over-carbonation or even bottle explosions.

Let the fermentation process continue until the bubbling stops or is very minimal and specific gravity readings remain stable.

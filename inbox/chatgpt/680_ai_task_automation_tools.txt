ASSISTANT:
There are several **AI agent builders** that do not require coding experience and can automate daily tasks for you. Here are some popular options:

---

### **1. Zapier**
- **Purpose**: Automates workflows between different apps.
- **Ease of Use**: No coding required, drag-and-drop interface.
- **Key Features**:
   - Connects 5,000+ apps like Gmail, Slack, Google Sheets, Trello, etc.
   - Allows you to create "Zaps" to trigger actions (e.g., automate email responses or move data between tools).
   - AI tools can now integrate with your tasks (e.g., using OpenAI GPT for content creation).

---

### **2. Make (formerly Integromat)**
- **Purpose**: Visual automation for workflows.
- **Ease of Use**: Beginner-friendly, highly visual.
- **Key Features**:
   - Powerful drag-and-drop interface for creating task automations.
   - Supports integration of multiple tools and APIs.
   - Allows you to create custom workflows for repetitive tasks.

---

### **3. Airtable Automations**
- **Purpose**: Organize tasks and automate simple workflows.
- **Ease of Use**: Minimal learning curve.
- **Key Features**:
   - Combines a database with no-code automation tools.
   - Automates processes like sending emails, updating records, or syncing data.
   - Integrates with Slack, Google Calendar, and other popular tools.

---

### **4. Microsoft Power Automate**
- **Purpose**: Automate workflows across Microsoft and third-party apps.
- **Ease of Use**: No coding required, ideal for Office 365 users.
- **Key Features**:
   - Pre-built templates for automating repetitive tasks.
   - Works seamlessly with Excel, Outlook, Teams, and more.
   - AI Builder enables AI-powered workflow automation without coding.

---

### **5. Bubble.io**
- **Purpose**: Build AI-powered apps and workflows without coding.
- **Ease of Use**: Intermediate (a bit more advanced but no coding).
- **Key Features**:
   - Drag-and-drop UI builder.
   - Can integrate AI tools (like OpenAI) to automate tasks.
   - Suitable for custom apps or workflows for small businesses.

---

### **6. Pipedream**
- **Purpose**: Automate workflows, integrate apps and APIs.
- **Ease of Use**: Low-code/no-code with simple interfaces.
- **Key Features**:
   - Connects apps like Gmail, Airtable, or ChatGPT.
   - Handles APIs and advanced automations without coding.

---

### **7. OpenAI’s GPTs (Custom GPTs)**
- **Purpose**: Use OpenAI’s GPT builder for AI-driven tasks.
- **Ease of Use**: Beginner-friendly, no code.
- **Key Features**:
   - You can create specialized GPTs to handle specific tasks.
   - Ideal for personal AI assistants that automate text-based tasks.

---

### **8. Taskade**
- **Purpose**: AI task and project automation.
- **Ease of Use**: Simple, designed for productivity.
- **Key Features**:
   - Combines task management with AI-powered workflows.
   - Automates repetitive content creation and task organization.

---

These tools allow you to **automate daily tasks**, improve efficiency, and integrate AI with little to no coding experience. I recommend starting with **Zapier** or **Make** for beginner-friendly automation. If you need help setting up workflows in any of these, let me know!

---

### **Workflow Requirements**  
- Retrieve emails containing AI news.  
- Filter emails by specific keywords (e.g., “AI News,” “Trends,” etc.).  
- Store the email content (Google Sheets, Notion, or other databases).  
- Optional: Send notifications or summaries.

---

### **Step-by-Step Workflow in Make**

#### **Step 1: Create a Scenario**  
1. Go to [Make.com](https://www.make.com) and log in.  
2. Click on **Create a New Scenario**.  

---

#### **Step 2: Connect the Email App**  
1. Select **Email** as the trigger.  
2. Choose the **IMAP** or **Gmail** module to connect your email provider.  
   - **For Gmail**:
     - Log in to your Gmail account and authorize access.  
   - **For IMAP**: Provide your email server settings (IMAP host, username, and password).  
3. Set the **Trigger Condition**:
   - Filter emails containing specific keywords like “AI News,” “AI Trends,” etc.  
   - Example Filter:  
     - Subject contains: **“AI News”**  
     - Body contains: **“Trends”**, **“Updates”**, etc.  

---

#### **Step 3: Retrieve and Parse Emails**  
1. Use the **Email > List Emails** module to pull relevant emails.  
2. Use a **Text Parser** module to extract specific details like:  
   - Sender Name  
   - Email Subject  
   - Email Body  
   - Date and Time  

---

#### **Step 4: Store the Emails in a Database**  
1. Choose a database or spreadsheet to store the extracted email data. Options:  
   - **Google Sheets**:  
     - Connect Google Sheets.  
     - Add rows with columns: **Sender, Subject, Content, Date, etc.**  
   - **Notion**:  
     - Create a table/database in Notion.  
     - Map extracted email details into your Notion database.  
   - **Airtable**:  
     - Create an Airtable table to store email data.  

---

#### **Step 5: Optional – Send Notifications**  
1. Add a **Slack** or **Telegram** module to send a notification when new AI news emails are retrieved.  
2. Include a short summary or the subject of the email.  

---

#### **Step 6: Set Up Filters and Run**  
1. Add a **filter** to ensure only relevant emails are processed.  
   - Example Condition: `Subject` contains **“AI News”**.  
2. Run the scenario manually to test it.  
3. Schedule the workflow:  
   - Set it to run every 5–15 minutes or daily as needed.  

---

### **Final Workflow in Make**  
1. **Trigger**: Email (IMAP/Gmail) – Retrieve New Emails.  
2. **Filter**: Filter emails by keywords (e.g., “AI News”).  
3. **Text Parser**: Extract key content (Subject, Sender, Body).  
4. **Action**: Store data in Google Sheets/Notion/Airtable.  
5. **Optional**: Send notifications via Slack/Telegram.

Here's a step-by-step workflow for **Make (formerly Integromat)** to integrate Gmail and retrieve emails containing **"The Rundown"**, **"TLDR"**, and **"The Neuron"**.

---

### **Workflow Steps in Make**

1. **Connect Gmail to Retrieve Emails**  
2. **Filter Emails with Specific Keywords**  
3. **Store the Emails in Google Sheets or a Database**  
4. **Run the Scenario Automatically**  

---

### **Step 1: Set Up Gmail Integration**
1. Go to **Make.com** and log in.  
2. Click **Create a new scenario**.  
3. In the scenario editor, search for **Gmail** and add the **Watch Emails** module.  
   - **Trigger**: Watch for new Gmail messages.  
   - Connect your Gmail account:
     - Log in with your Gmail credentials and grant permission.  

---

### **Step 2: Configure Gmail Module**
1. **Set the Folder/Label**: Choose “Inbox” (or a specific label if you have filtered these newsletters into one).  
2. **Search Query**: Use Gmail’s search operators to filter emails containing the desired keywords:  
   ```
   subject:"The Rundown" OR subject:"TLDR" OR subject:"The Neuron"
   ```
3. Set the **Limit** for the number of emails retrieved per run (e.g., 5, 10).  

---

### **Step 3: Add a Filter to Refine Results**
1. Use a **Filter** between modules to ensure only relevant emails proceed.  
2. Condition:  
   - `Subject` contains "The Rundown"  
   - OR `Subject` contains "TLDR"  
   - OR `Subject` contains "The Neuron"  

---

### **Step 4: Extract Email Content**
1. Add a **Text Parser** module to extract:  
   - **Subject**: Title of the newsletter.  
   - **Body**: Main content.  
   - **Date**: Date received.  
2. Optionally, include **Sender Name** or **Sender Email**.

---

### **Step 5: Store Emails in Google Sheets**
1. Add the **Google Sheets > Add Row** module:  
   - Connect Google Sheets to Make.  
   - Select the Google Sheets file where you want to store the data.  
   - Map the extracted fields to columns in your sheet:  
     - **Column A**: Email Subject  
     - **Column B**: Sender  
     - **Column C**: Date  
     - **Column D**: Email Body  

---

### **Step 6: Schedule the Scenario**
1. Set the **Scenario Schedule** to run automatically:  
   - Every **15 minutes** or at a frequency that suits you.  

---

### **Final Workflow Summary**
1. **Gmail - Watch Emails**: Retrieve emails containing “The Rundown,” “TLDR,” and “The Neuron.”  
2. **Filter**: Ensure only relevant newsletters are processed.  
3. **Text Parser**: Extract Subject, Body, Date, and Sender.  
4. **Google Sheets - Add Row**: Save email details into a spreadsheet.

### Event Overview:

ASSISTANT:
Here are some tailored recommendations based on the analysis of the "FL Records Management Association Agreement":

### 1. **Room Block and Attrition**:
   - **Review the Attrition Clause**: Ensure that the attrition penalties are clearly understood, especially in terms of how they are calculated if the room block is not met. Negotiate a higher allowable attrition percentage (e.g., 20% instead of the standard 10%) to provide more flexibility in case of lower attendance.
   - **Room Block Review Dates**: Request periodic room block reviews to adjust the room commitment closer to the event date if registration numbers vary, especially in the post-pandemic environment where event attendance can fluctuate.

### 2. **Cancellation Flexibility**:
   - **Negotiate a More Favorable Cancellation Policy**: Given the time frame until the event, it’s worth negotiating a more flexible sliding scale for cancellation penalties. The current structure may impose significant financial risk closer to the event. Consider securing a clause that reduces the cancellation penalty in the event of force majeure or unforeseen market conditions.
   - **Force Majeure**: Ensure that the force majeure clause includes specific language for pandemics and other public health issues to allow for cancellation or rescheduling without penalties.

### 3. **Food and Beverage Minimum**:
   - **Lower or Waive the F&B Minimum**: A $45,000 F&B minimum may be high, depending on the projected attendance and their consumption habits. Negotiate for a reduction in the minimum or request a more flexible F&B arrangement where non-consumed amounts could be applied to other services, such as meeting space rental or AV services.
   - **Ensure Tax & Gratuity Clarity**: Make sure that the contract specifies whether the F&B minimum includes or excludes taxes, gratuities, and service charges. Negotiate for flexibility in case actual consumption falls short.

### 4. **AV Provider and Surcharges**:
   - **Outside AV Vendor Charges**: If you prefer to use an outside AV vendor, negotiate to either waive or minimize any hotel-imposed surcharges for external AV providers. If possible, secure a flat rate rather than percentage-based fees for AV services.
   - **Ensure Competitive Pricing for AV**: Ask for detailed pricing for in-house AV services to compare with potential outside vendors. Request a clause allowing flexibility to switch vendors if the service or pricing is unfavorable.

### 5. **Complimentary Amenities and Concessions**:
   - **Negotiate Additional Concessions**: Request additional concessions from the hotel, such as complimentary room upgrades for key staff, waived parking fees, or reduced resort fees for attendees. These additional concessions can offset other potential costs.
   - **Adjustable Concessions**: Secure a clause that allows concessions to be adjusted proportionally if the room block or F&B minimums are not met. This provides protection in case the final attendance is lower than anticipated.

### 6. **Deposit and Payment Terms**:
   - **Staggered Payment Schedule**: Ensure the deposit schedule is fair and that payments are staggered according to event milestones, such as a percentage due at contract signing, six months before the event, and final payment closer to the event date.
   - **Final Review of Deposit Terms**: Review and negotiate the deposit schedule to avoid locking up too much capital upfront. It may be beneficial to negotiate lower initial deposits or longer timeframes between payments.

### 7. **Force Majeure and COVID-19 Language**:
   - **Ensure COVID-19 Coverage**: Given the uncertainty around future public health concerns, negotiate specific force majeure language that includes COVID-19 or similar health threats as valid reasons for cancellation without penalty.
   - **Right to Rebook**: Secure a clause that allows for event rescheduling within a reasonable time frame if cancellation occurs due to force majeure, without losing deposits or facing penalties.

### 8. **Governing Law and Dispute Resolution**:
   - **Review Dispute Resolution Clause**: Confirm that the arbitration process is fair and negotiate for the inclusion of a mediation step before arbitration, which can often lead to quicker and less expensive resolutions.
   - **Waiver of Jury Trial**: If this clause is concerning, you may want to negotiate to retain the right to a jury trial, especially if there are concerns about the potential for disputes.

### 9. **Audit Clause**:
   - **Room Pickup Audit**: Negotiate for a room audit clause that allows the client to compare room pickup reports with the hotel’s data to ensure accuracy, particularly for attrition calculations.

### 10. **Incorporate a Courtesy Hold**:
   - **Request Courtesy Hold**: Negotiate for the hotel to hold a certain number of additional rooms on a “courtesy hold” basis, especially if you anticipate that the event could grow in size. This will ensure availability without a financial commitment upfront.

These recommendations will help ensure that your client's interests are protected while securing the flexibility needed to manage event logistics effectively.

ASSISTANT:
A **tariff** is a tax that a government places on goods imported from other countries. It makes those imported products more expensive for buyers in the country.

### How tariffs affect imports and prices:

1. **Higher Prices for Imported Goods** – Since businesses have to pay the tariff on top of the product cost, they often pass that cost to consumers, making imported goods more expensive.

2. **Less Competition for Local Businesses** – Because imported goods become pricier, people may buy more locally made products, helping domestic industries grow.

3. **Possible Retaliation from Other Countries** – If one country imposes tariffs, other countries might respond by placing their own tariffs, making it harder to sell goods internationally.

4. **Reduced Choice for Consumers** – If tariffs make imports too expensive, fewer foreign goods are available, limiting choices for buyers.

5. **More Government Revenue** – Tariffs generate money for the government, which can be used for public services or infrastructure.

In short, tariffs protect local industries but can make products more expensive for consumers and sometimes lead to trade conflicts.

ASSISTANT:
The Pearson-Marr Archetype Indicator (PMAI) is a psychological tool used to help people discover which universal patterns, or archetypes, most influence their personality, thoughts, and behavior. It is based on the idea that we all have different archetypes within us, like "The Hero," "The Caregiver," or "The Creator." These archetypes represent common human themes that can shape how we see the world, make decisions, and approach challenges.

The PMAI works by having individuals answer a series of questions. Based on their answers, the test reveals their strongest archetypes, giving insight into their personal strengths, motivations, and challenges. It’s often used for personal growth, self-awareness, and even in professional development to better understand oneself and others.

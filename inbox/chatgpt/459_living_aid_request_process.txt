ASSISTANT:
The lease agreement **does not contain a specific clause titled "Living Aid" or "Live-in Aide"**. However, the right to request a live-in aide is typically protected under **HUD regulations** and **fair housing laws** for tenants with disabilities.

**Relevant clause:**

- **Section 22: Prohibition of Discrimination** — explicitly states that the Owner shall not discriminate against any person because of **disability** in connection with the lease.

While the lease doesn't list live-in aide policies, **you have a right to request a reasonable accommodation**, such as allowing a live-in aide, if you have a documented need due to a disability. This is covered under the **Fair Housing Act** and **HUD regulations** for the Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) program.

To proceed:
1. Submit a **written reasonable accommodation request** to the Housing Authority (HACP).
2. Include a **medical statement or verification** of your need for a live-in aide.
3. The aide **must not be part of your household** and **must not be obligated for rent**.

What happens if I need to change my live in aid

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
If you need to **change your live-in aide**, the following process applies under HUD and HACP rules—even if it's not explicitly stated in the lease:

---

### **Step-by-Step Process to Change a Live-In Aide:**

1. **Notify HACP in writing**
   - State that you want to **remove the current aide** and request approval for a **new aide**.
   - Include the reason for the change (optional unless requested).

2. **Submit documentation for the new aide**
   - Name, date of birth, and identification.
   - Background check and screening may be required (HACP usually screens aides for criminal history or program violations).

3. **Verify continued need for the aide**
   - A doctor’s letter or verification from a licensed professional may be requested again to confirm your ongoing need.

4. **Wait for HACP approval**
   - The new aide cannot move in until officially approved.

5. **Update the lease record**
   - While the aide isn't added as a household member, the landlord may want updated contact info or to note the change.

---

### **Important Notes:**
- A live-in aide **has no tenancy rights**.
- If you remove the aide, they must **vacate the unit** immediately.
- The new aide must **not contribute to rent** or income.

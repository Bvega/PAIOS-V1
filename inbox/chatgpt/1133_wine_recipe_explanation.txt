1-1/2 Teaspoon of Acid Blend.
1/4 Teaspoon of Wine Tannin.
1/8 Teaspoon of Ascorbic Acid.
1 Campden Tablet (crushed and dissolved)"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
This recipe appears to be for making a type of homemade wine or similar fermented beverage. Here’s an explanation of each ingredient and its purpose:

1. **1 Pound of Cane Sugar**: Cane sugar provides the fermentable sugars that yeast will convert into alcohol during fermentation.

2. **1 Teaspoon of Yeast Nutrient**: Yeast nutrient supplies essential nutrients that yeast needs to thrive and perform a healthy fermentation. It typically contains nitrogen, vitamins, and minerals.

3. **1/8 Teaspoon of Pectic Enzyme**: Pectic enzyme breaks down pectin, a substance found in fruit that can cause haze in the final product. It helps to clear the wine and can also increase juice yield from fruit.

4. **1-1/2 Teaspoons of Acid Blend**: Acid blend is a mixture of acids (usually citric, malic, and tartaric acids) that balance the flavor and pH of the wine, ensuring a good taste and aiding in preservation.

5. **1/4 Teaspoon of Wine Tannin**: Tannin adds astringency and complexity to the wine. It comes from grape skins, seeds, and stems and contributes to the wine's structure and aging potential.

6. **1/8 Teaspoon of Ascorbic Acid**: Ascorbic acid, also known as Vitamin C, acts as an antioxidant, preventing oxidation and helping to preserve the color and flavor of the wine.

7. **1 Campden Tablet (crushed and dissolved)**: Campden tablets contain potassium metabisulfite, which sterilizes the must (unfermented wine) by killing wild yeast, bacteria, and other unwanted microorganisms. This helps ensure a controlled fermentation with the desired yeast.

These ingredients are typically mixed with water and sometimes fruit or juice to create the must, which is then fermented to produce wine.

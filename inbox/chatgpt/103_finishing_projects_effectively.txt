Based on all our interactions, I see a clear pattern: you generate a lot of valuable ideas and jump fast into execution, but you also leave some projects half-finished because a new idea grabs your attention. That’s a strength (creativity, initiative, energy) but it becomes a weakness if follow-through is missing. Here’s a structure I’d recommend to help you finish and follow up properly:

---

## 1. Capture & Park New Ideas
- Keep a single **“Idea Bank” document** (Notion, Google Docs, or even a simple TXT).  
- Every time a new idea comes, write it down there with a date and 3-5 bullet points.  
- This way, you don’t lose the spark, but you don’t derail your current focus.

---

## 2. Prioritize Ongoing Projects
- Make a **shortlist of 3 active projects** max.  
- Rank them using criteria:  
  - **Impact** (what moves you forward the most in career/business?)  
  - **Feasibility** (what can realistically be finished soon?)  
  - **Alignment** (what connects to your long-term vision?).  

The project that scores highest becomes **priority #1**.

---

## 3. Define “Done” Clearly
For each project, write down:  
- **Goal**: one clear outcome that means it’s finished.  
- **Deliverable**: tangible file, deployment, presentation, or document.  
- **Deadline**: realistic but strict.  

This avoids the “open loop” feeling that makes you jump away.

---

## 4. Work in Stages
Break your project into **stages** (like you did with your capstone: Stage 1 backend, Stage 2 frontend, etc.).  
- Each stage = one milestone + one backup/ZIP.  
- Celebrate finishing stages; it gives momentum and closure.  

---

## 5. Weekly Review & Follow-Up
- Once a week, check:  
  - What moved forward?  
  - What got stuck?  
  - Which idea in the “bank” should be revisited later?  

This keeps everything visible and prevents forgotten projects.

---

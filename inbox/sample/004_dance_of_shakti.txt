If you mean **“the dance of Shakti”**, it refers to the *cosmic, creative, feminine energy* in Hindu philosophy.

### **Short answer**
**The Dance of Shakti** is the eternal movement of energy that creates, sustains, and transforms the universe. It represents life force, intuition, power, and feminine divinity in motion.

### **More detail**
- In Hindu tradition, **Shakti** is the divine feminine energy behind all existence.  
- Her “dance” symbolizes:
  - **Creation** (birthing the universe)  
  - **Preservation** (maintaining harmony)  
  - **Destruction / Transformation** (clearing for new cycles)  
- It complements **Shiva’s Tandava dance**, which represents cosmic cycles. Together, Shiva (pure consciousness) and Shakti (dynamic energy) create universal balance.

### If you meant **Shakti Dance (modern practice)**  
There is also a contemporary spiritual practice called **Shakti Dance®**, created by Sara Avtar:
- It blends **yoga**, **fluid dance**, **mantra**, and **meditation**  
- Designed to awaken feminine energy, intuition, and creative flow  
- Often practiced in Kundalini yoga communities

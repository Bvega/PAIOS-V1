ASSISTANT:
To address this denial, you can take the following steps:

### 1. **Review the Denial Letter Carefully**
   - The denial is based on Highmark Blue Shield’s requirement that your **coronary artery calcium (CAC) score** must be **300 Agatston units or higher**.
   - The insurance company determined that the medication **Repatha** was **not medically necessary** under their policy.

### 2. **Contact Your Doctor**
   - **Discuss with your doctor** whether your CAC score meets or exceeds the 300 Agatston threshold.
   - If your score is **close to 300**, your doctor may be able to provide additional medical evidence or justification.

### 3. **Request an Appeal (First-Level Appeal)**
   - **Your doctor can submit an appeal** explaining why Repatha is medically necessary for your condition.
   - They should **include supporting medical records**, such as:
     - A history of failed treatments with other cholesterol-lowering medications.
     - Evidence of worsening cardiovascular risk or progressive atherosclerosis.
     - Any other risk factors that justify the use of Repatha.
   - If your CAC score is **below 300**, your doctor can argue that strict adherence to this policy is inappropriate in your case.

### 4. **Request a Peer-to-Peer Review**
   - Your doctor can **request a peer-to-peer review** with the insurance company’s medical reviewer.
   - This allows a direct conversation where your physician can explain why Repatha is essential.

### 5. **File a Second-Level Appeal**
   - If the first appeal is denied, you have the right to **file a second-level appeal** with more supporting documentation.
   - If necessary, consider **seeking a specialist’s second opinion** (such as a cardiologist) to strengthen your case.

### 6. **External Review (if Appeals Fail)**
   - If all internal appeals are denied, you may request an **external review** by an independent third party.
   - Your state insurance commissioner or an external medical board will assess whether the denial is justified.

### 7. **Explore Patient Assistance Programs**
   - **Amgen (manufacturer of Repatha) offers a patient assistance program** that may help cover costs if insurance denies the claim.
   - Visit [Amgen’s Repatha Patient Assistance Program](https://www.repatha.com) or call their support line.

### 8. **Consider Legal Assistance**
   - If you believe the insurance denial is unfair and violating coverage policies, consider consulting a **healthcare attorney** or **patient advocacy group**.

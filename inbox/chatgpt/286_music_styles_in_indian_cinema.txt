Here's a breakdown of the **music styles**, **moods**, and **lyrical themes** across the **Hindu, Telugu, and Hindi music industries** (focused on the film and devotional contexts in India):

---

### 🎼 **Music Styles**

| Style                        | Description                                                                 |
|-----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Classical**               | Includes *Carnatic* (South India, Telugu) and *Hindustani* (North India, Hindi). |
| **Folk**                    | Regional traditions like *Lavani* (Maharashtra), *Baul* (Bengal), *Janapada* (Telugu). |
| **Film (Cinematic)**        | Dominates Telugu and Hindi industries (Tollywood and Bollywood). Blends classical, electronic, pop, rock. |
| **Devotional (Bhakti)**     | Includes *bhajans*, *keerthanas*, *slokas*, focused on gods like Krishna, Shiva, etc. Common in all three. |
| **Indipop / Indie**         | Independent music scene, non-film, growing rapidly with artists like Armaan Malik, Prateek Kuhad. |
| **Sufi**                    | Mystic Islamic influence, often in Hindi/Urdu songs (*Khwaja Mere Khwaja*, *Kun Faya Kun*). |
| **Qawwali**                 | Devotional form but with a strong Sufi-Islamic root. Performed in Hindi/Urdu often. |
| **Dance / Item Numbers**    | High-energy songs, big in Bollywood and Tollywood movies, used in club scenes or promotional numbers. |
| **Remix / EDM fusion**      | Classic songs reworked with electronic beats—popular in both industries now. |
| **Rock / Fusion / Jazz**    | Bands and composers experiment by blending Indian and Western styles. AR Rahman pioneered this. |

---

### 🎭 **Common Moods / Emotions (Rasas)**

| Mood               | Description and Examples                              |
|--------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| **Romantic (Shringara)**   | *Tum Hi Ho*, *Kal Ho Naa Ho*, *Samajavaragamana* |
| **Devotional (Bhakti)**    | *Om Jai Jagdish Hare*, *Govinda Govinda*, *Annamayya Keerthanalu* |
| **Melancholic (Karuna)**   | *Tadap Tadap Ke*, *Idhigo Rayalaseema*, *Nuvvunte Naa Jathagaa* |
| **Energetic / Celebration**| *Malhari*, *Ramuloo Ramulaa*, *London Thumakda* |
| **Heroic (Veeram)**        | *Jai Ho*, *Sye Raa Title Song*, *Zinda* |
| **Spiritual / Philosophical** | *Aarti*, *Bhaja Govindam*, *O Paalanhaare*, *Oke Oka Jeevitham* |
| **Patriotic**              | *Vande Mataram*, *Des Rangila*, *Bharat Ane Nenu* |
| **Tragic Love / Loss**     | *Kabira*, *Tujhe Bhula Diya*, *Yevadu Evadu* |

---

### ✍️ **Lyrical Themes**

| Theme                  | Notes & Example Songs |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Love & Heartbreak**  | The most dominant theme in both Hindi and Telugu music. |
| **Spiritual Devotion** | Songs praising deities like Rama, Krishna, Shiva, Ganesha, Durga. Common in Hindu devotional music. |
| **Motherland / Patriotism** | Seen in Republic Day/Independence Day specials. |
| **Social Messages**    | Lyrics on caste, poverty, social reform (*Rang De Basanti*, *Arjun Reddy* soundtrack). |
| **Philosophical Wisdom** | Ephemeral nature of life, karma, dharma (*Luka Chuppi*, *Maa Tujhe Salaam*). |
| **Friendship / Brotherhood** | *Dil Chahta Hai*, *Mustafa Mustafa*, *Naa Peru Surya* |
| **Festivals / Celebrations** | Holi, Diwali, Sankranthi songs are huge: *Balam Pichkari*, *Pandaga Chesko*. |

---

### 🗣️ Language Distribution

| Language  | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| **Hindi** | Bollywood, pan-India audience. |
| **Telugu** | Dominant in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana. Big film industry (Tollywood). |
| **Sanskrit / Tamil / Kannada / Marathi** | Used for classical/devotional crossover pieces. |

---

### 🔊 Notable Composers

- **AR Rahman** – Pan-Indian legend, blends styles.
- **Ilaiyaraaja** – Southern legend, rich orchestration.
- **Devi Sri Prasad** – Telugu mass hits, energetic.
- **MM Keeravani** – *Baahubali*, devotional scores.
- **Pritam / Vishal-Shekhar / Amit Trivedi** – Hindi film trendsetters.

The closer the cancellation is to the event date, the higher the percentage of the anticipated revenue that the Group will owe, reaching up to 90% if canceled within 90 days of the event. This could result in substantial costs if the event needs to be canceled.

3. **Concessions Dependency**: The concessions offered, such as complimentary rooms and discounts, are contingent on achieving at least 80% of anticipated sleeping room revenue and food and beverage revenue. Falling short of this threshold will proportionately reduce the concessions, increasing costs for the Group.

4. **Food and Beverage Commitment**: A minimum food and beverage revenue of $45,000 is required. Failure to meet this minimum will result in charges equivalent to the shortfall, which can be a significant risk if the event does not attract the anticipated number of attendees.

5. **Force Majeure Limitations**: The force majeure clause covers various unforeseen circumstances but notably excludes the ongoing or anticipated impact of COVID-19 unless specific new travel warnings are issued. This limitation could pose a risk if circumstances related to the pandemic affect the event.

6. **Indemnification and Liability**: The Group is required to indemnify the Hotel for various claims arising out of its negligence or willful misconduct. This places a substantial liability burden on the Group, requiring careful management of event logistics and attendee behavior.

7. **Payment Terms**: The contract stipulates strict payment deadlines and requirements for deposits and guarantees. Failure to meet these terms could result in the cancellation of reserved space, with the Group still liable for cancellation fees.

- As detailed in the risks section, the attrition clause allows for 20% slippage but imposes penalties if the Group falls below the 80% threshold of the contracted room block. The hotel agrees to make reasonable efforts to resell any unused rooms, which could mitigate some of the attrition fees.

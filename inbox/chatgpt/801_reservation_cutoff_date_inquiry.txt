What clauses are missing?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
Based on a review of typical hotel event contracts, here are some standard clauses that are not explicitly outlined in the **Draft Agreement - Bunzl - 2025 Safety Leadership Meeting** and may enhance clarity, protection, and flexibility:

### 1. **Force Majeure Clause**
   - This clause addresses unforeseen events (e.g., natural disasters, pandemics) that may prevent the event from occurring. It provides the group and hotel relief from liability if the event is canceled due to circumstances beyond their control. 

### 2. **Rebooking Clause**
   - This allows the group to apply a portion of any cancellation fees toward a future booking within a specified timeframe, often within one year. This is especially useful if an event needs to be canceled but could be rescheduled.

### 3. **Indemnification Clause**
   - This clause clarifies the responsibilities for indemnification, ensuring that each party holds the other harmless for liabilities resulting from their actions. It's standard for event contracts to outline indemnification for personal injury or property damage occurring during the event.

### 4. **ADA Compliance**
   - Including an ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) compliance clause ensures the hotel will meet accessibility needs for attendees with disabilities. This specifies both public accommodation and group responsibilities.

### 5. **Confidentiality Clause**
   - This would protect proprietary and confidential information shared between the hotel and the group, such as room rates, concessions, or other sensitive data. It’s beneficial, especially if sensitive information is exchanged during negotiations.

### 6. **Insurance Requirements**
   - Specifies minimum insurance requirements for both the hotel and the group. This might include general liability, property, and workers' compensation insurance and ensure that both parties are adequately covered.

### 7. **Quiet Enjoyment / No Disturbance**
   - A quiet enjoyment clause guarantees that there will be no scheduled disturbances (e.g., construction, renovation, loud events) that could disrupt the event or the guest rooms during the contract dates.

### 8. **Additional Spend Limitations**
   - This clause restricts the group’s financial liability for extra services (like AV, F&B) requested by attendees unless pre-approved by an authorized group representative. It’s useful to prevent unanticipated charges.

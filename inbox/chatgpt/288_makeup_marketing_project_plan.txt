ASSISTANT:
Here’s how I’d build this as my own business, from validation through scale:

1. **Validate the Opportunity**  
   - Run a quick survey or informal interviews with makeup buyers to see where they struggle (out-of-stock, fragmented suppliers, price hunting).  
   - Analyze competitors (other aggregators, marketplaces, affiliate boutiques) to spot gaps—maybe focus on indie or cruelty-free brands.  

2. **Secure Supplier & Affiliate Partnerships**  
   - Apply for Amazon’s Product Advertising API and any other major retailers (e.g. Ulta, Sephora, Beautylish).  
   - Negotiate direct affiliate or reseller agreements with niche suppliers who aren’t well represented on Amazon.  

3. **Define Your MVP**  
   - A simple web app that:  
     1. Aggregates live inventory and prices across your APIs  
     2. Lets users filter by brand, price, availability  
     3. Lets users submit an order request that you fulfill on their behalf  
   - Keep UI minimal—product grid + detail modal + “request order” form.  

4. **Build the Tech Stack**  
   - **Frontend**: Next.js (for SSR / SEO-friendly pages) + Tailwind CSS for rapid styling  
   - **Backend**: Node.js/Express microservice to handle API polling + caching + order orchestration  
   - **Database**: PostgreSQL for products, suppliers, users, orders  
   - **Hosting**: Vercel for frontend; Heroku or DigitalOcean for backend  

5. **Implement Real-Time Aggregation**  
   - Write a service that polls each supplier API on a schedule (e.g. every 5–15 minutes), caches results in your DB, and invalidates stale data.  
   - Expose a unified “/products” endpoint that merges feeds, dedupes SKUs, and ranks by availability or margin.  

6. **Order Management & Payments**  
   - Integrate Stripe or PayPal so customers can pre-pay or pay a small deposit.  
   - Build an admin dashboard where you see incoming requests, supplier cost, customer info, and “purchase” buttons that route to the supplier checkout flow.  
   - Automate email confirmations and shipping updates.  

7. **Beta Launch & Feedback Loop**  
   - Recruit 20–50 makeup enthusiasts to test—offer them free shipping or a small discount for feedback.  
   - Track qualitative feedback (UX frustrations, missing features) and quantitative metrics (time to find product, conversion rate).  

8. **Marketing & Growth**  
   - **SEO & Content**: Publish ingredient-deep-dives, how-to tutorials, and “best of” lists targeting long-tail beauty queries.  
   - **Influencers & Affiliates**: Partner with micro-influencers to drive traffic via honest reviews using your platform.  
   - **Email & SMS Campaigns**: Build drip sequences around abandoned requests (“Still looking for that highlighter?”).  

9. **Measure & Iterate**  
   - Monitor key metrics: conversion rate, average order value, time on site, churn of requests (unfulfilled vs. completed).  
   - A/B test homepage layouts, filter defaults, and promotional copy.  

10. **Scale**  
   - Onboard more suppliers (indie, international) and add features like wish lists, price-drop alerts, and loyalty rewards.  
   - Expand beyond makeup into skincare or haircare verticals once your core workflow is solid.  

By moving systematically—from validating shopper pain points to rolling out a lean MVP, then layering in marketing and operational rigor—you build both a product and a brand that customers trust to save them time and money.

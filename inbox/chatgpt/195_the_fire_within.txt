ASSISTANT:
Not exactly—**tokens** and **letters** are related but different:

- A **token** is typically a **word or part of a word** that an AI model uses to understand and generate text. It might include:
  - A short word like “cat” = 1 token
  - A longer word like “walking” = 2 tokens (“walk” + “ing”)
  - Punctuation and spaces can also count as tokens

- A **letter** is just a single **character** (A, B, C, etc.), so it's a much smaller unit than a token.

**Example:**  
The sentence: “I wake each day in borrowed light.”  
- Has **33 characters (letters + spaces + punctuation)**
- Has **9 tokens**

So 1,000 tokens might equal roughly **750–900 words** or **4,000–5,000 characters**, depending on the text.

ASSISTANT:
After reviewing the Attrition clauses across the provided contract models and comparing them to best practices in the clause library, here are the **high-risk terms** identified:

---

### **1. No Attrition Penalty Clauses (Contract Model 002 Original / Anonymized Contract 001)**
> *“There will be no attrition penalties if the group utilizes less than the contracted block.”*【16†Contract model 002 original.docx】

**Risk:**  
- **Hotel Exposure**: This clause heavily favors the group. The hotel bears the full risk of unbooked rooms, with no compensation.
- **Revenue Uncertainty**: The hotel cannot recoup losses from unutilized inventory.
- **Recommendation**: Re-introduce a structured attrition schedule aligned with industry standards (typically 80–90% minimum pickup, with liquidated damages for the shortfall).

---

### **2. Fixed Attrition Percentage Without Room Resale Offset (Contact Model 001 Original & Final)**
> *“If actual block consumption falls below 80%…the difference will be charged…as liquidated damages.”*【23†contact model 001 original.docx】

**Risk:**
- **No Offset Language**: There is no clause to reduce damages based on rooms resold by the hotel.
- **Non-Adjustable**: No mention of mid-point review or re-negotiation opportunities.
- **Recommendation**: Include a resale credit provision and room block review window at 60 or 90 days prior to arrival.

---

### **3. Rigid Liquidated Damages Trigger (Anonymized Contract 002 & Contract Model 002 Final)**
> *“Should Group fall below this amount Group will be responsible for the difference to meet 80% or 105 room nights…”*【22†Anonymized_Contract002.docx】【24†Contract model 002 final.docx】

**Risk:**
- **Strict Trigger**: Automatically imposes liability if threshold isn’t met, regardless of mitigation.
- **No carve-outs**: Doesn’t account for unforeseen changes in attendance or hotel re-sale success.
- **Recommendation**: Include mutual renegotiation language and soften the enforcement with a force majeure carve-out or documented good faith effort clause.

---

### **4. Concession Forfeiture for Attrition (Contract Model 003)**
> *“…special considerations are predicated on a minimum guest room pick-up…Should pickup fall below this number, special considerations may be adjusted…”*【21†Contract model 003 final.docx】

**Risk:**
- **Unilateral Hotel Discretion**: Hotel has authority to revoke concessions based on group pickup.
- **Ambiguity**: Terms like “may be adjusted” are vague and give the hotel undue leverage.
- **Recommendation**: Define “adjusted” and ensure any revocation of concessions is proportional and mutually agreed upon.

---

### **Summary of High-Risk Elements:**
| Risk Type                         | Clause Location(s)                                       | Recommended Fix                                                  |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| No attrition penalty             | Contract Model 002 Original                              | Reintroduce a standard attrition clause                         |
| No room resale offset            | Contact Model 001 Original & Final                       | Add clause crediting rooms resold by the hotel                 |
| Strict liquidated damages clause | Anonymized Contract 002, Contract Model 002 Final        | Include force majeure or re-evaluation clauses                 |
| Unilateral concession removal    | Contract Model 003 Original/Final                        | Clarify and mutually define concession adjustments             |

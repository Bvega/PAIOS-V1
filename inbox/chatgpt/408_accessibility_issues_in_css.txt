Here are the **accessibility faults** identified in the provided CSS code:

---

### ⚠️ **Accessibility Issues**

- **Missing focus styles**
  - Interactive elements like `.contact-submit-button` have no `:focus` state defined. Keyboard users won’t see where they are navigating.
  - ✅ **Fix**: Add `outline`, `box-shadow`, or other visible indicators for `:focus`.

---

- **Low color contrast**
  - `.contact-input::-webkit-input-placeholder, .contact-textarea::-webkit-input-placeholder { color: #aaaaaa; }`  
    Against a white background, `#aaaaaa` may not meet WCAG minimum contrast ratio (4.5:1) for normal text.
  - ✅ **Fix**: Use a darker gray (e.g., `#757575` or darker).

---

- **Text resizing and responsiveness**
  - Fixed pixel widths (`width: 300px`, `width: 800px`) and font sizes (`font-size: 48px`, `font-size: 20px`) can cause problems for users who increase text size or zoom.
  - ✅ **Fix**: Use relative units (em, rem, %, vw) for font sizes and layout widths.

---

- **Image rotation and overflow**
  - `.contact-image { transform: rotate(90deg); width: 900px; margin-left: -60px; }`  
    This rotated and oversized image may confuse screen magnifier users and cause scroll traps.
  - ✅ **Fix**: Ensure image layout is responsive and visually coherent at all sizes.

---

- **No indication of required fields**
  - Fields like `.contact-input` and `.contact-textarea` don’t indicate if they are required or optional.
  - ✅ **Fix**: Add `aria-required="true"` in HTML or visibly mark required fields (e.g., asterisk `*`).

---

- **No label association**
  - No indication in CSS that inputs are tied to `<label>` elements. This impairs screen reader navigation.
  - ✅ **Fix**: Ensure inputs are linked to `<label for="">` elements in HTML.

---

- **Unclear button purpose**
  - `.contact-submit-button` is styled but has no semantic enhancement (e.g., icon, label clarity, confirmation).
  - ✅ **Fix**: Use ARIA labels (e.g., `aria-label="Send message"`) and ensure visible text is clear and meaningful.

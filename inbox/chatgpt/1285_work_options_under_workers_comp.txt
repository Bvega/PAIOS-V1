What kind of work can you do under workers comp without affecting the beneficts

ASSISTANT:
Under workers' compensation, the type of work you can do without affecting your benefits depends on your specific situation and the laws in your jurisdiction. Generally, you are allowed to engage in certain types of work as long as it does not interfere with your recovery or contradict the terms of your workers' compensation agreement. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Light Duty Work**: If your doctor has cleared you for light-duty work, you can take on tasks that do not exacerbate your injury. This type of work is often arranged by your employer to accommodate your limitations.

2. **Part-Time Work**: In some cases, you may be able to work part-time while receiving workers' compensation benefits. However, any income you earn may be deducted from your benefits.

3. **Modified Duties**: You may perform modified duties that are less physically demanding than your regular job. This must be approved by your physician and employer.

4. **Non-Physical Work**: If your injury prevents you from performing physical tasks, you might be able to take on a job that involves administrative or clerical work.

5. **Vocational Rehabilitation**: Participation in vocational rehabilitation programs to help you transition to a different type of work is often encouraged and should not affect your benefits.

6. **Volunteering**: Engaging in volunteer work that does not interfere with your recovery might be permissible, but it's important to disclose this to your workers' compensation insurer.

It's crucial to keep in mind that all work activities should be reported to your workers' compensation insurer to avoid allegations of fraud. Always consult with your workers' compensation attorney or your claims adjuster to understand the specific rules and regulations that apply to your situation.

ASSISTANT:
Mostly — the meaning is fine, but there are small grammar/format issues.  
Problems:  
- **"clients resource"** should be either **"client resource"** (common compound modifier) or **"clients' resource"** (with apostrophe) — not *clients resource* without punctuation.  
- **"sought after"** should be hyphenated as **"sought-after"**.  
- Optionally remove redundancy **"most highly"** → use **"most respected"** or **"highly respected."**

Corrected versions you can use:

1) Minimal fix (keeps original wording):  
HelmsBriscoe's mission is to be the most highly respected, trusted and sought-after client resource firm worldwide. We are committed to exceeding the expectations of our meeting and travel partners by providing excellent value and exceptional customer service.

2) Polished version (cleaner):  
HelmsBriscoe's mission is to be the most respected, trusted, and sought-after client-resource firm worldwide. We are committed to exceeding the expectations of our meeting and travel partners by providing excellent value and exceptional customer service.

Either of these would be grammatically correct and clearer. Which style do you prefer?

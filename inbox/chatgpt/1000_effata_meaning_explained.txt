"Effata" is a Latin word meaning "be opened." It is often used in a religious context, particularly in Christianity, where it appears in the Gospel of Mark 7:34, during the miracle where Jesus heals a deaf man with a speech impediment by saying, "Ephphatha," which means "be opened." This phrase symbolizes the opening of ears and loosening of tongues, but it can also be interpreted more broadly as an invitation to be open to new experiences, understanding, and spiritual insight.

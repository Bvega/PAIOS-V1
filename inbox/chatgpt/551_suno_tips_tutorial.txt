Back to Tenacity:

First choose your output in the settings so you can hear anything. Drop your song into the program.

At first it looks like you can not do much and add only a few effects. You first have to select your song or the part of it which you will change.

To select the complete song you can press Ctrl+A and now its marked.

Now you can use the equalizer in the effects tab. I use the filter curve one.

Please notice you can just listen to the first few seconds of the marked song to hear the differnce. If you wanna hear some parts you have to mark them with your mouse before.

And you should only apply the changes if you wanna change your songs. The Equalizer will keep your changes if you close it. So no worry.

(If you always use apply he will always revise your song until it oversteers at some point.)

 

Now we have to go more into detail.

Let’s see what all the controls do:

 

Subbass or deep bass: anything below 60 Hz

Bass: 60-150 Hz - anything higher is no longer bass, regardless of the instrument.

Low/Lower Midrange: 150-800 Hz

Mids: everything around 1 kHz, but this is particularly controversial among musicians

High/upper midrange: above 1.6 kHz, up to 3 kHz

Treble: from 3 kHz mark

Airy highs/super high frequency range: everything above 14 kHz

 

Voices are mostly between 250hz (Female) and 350hz (Male). Raise 3-6khz in addition to optimize the vocals.

 

Kick-Drum:

50-100 Hz for more depth bass

150-250 Hz for Mid, If Mid is already high you can go down with 300-600 Hz

2-4 Khz for overall higher and heavy Drum sound (mostly for Rock songs)

 

Snare-Drum:

150 Hz you boost a little if the snare doesn't have enough power in the mix.

500 Hz is a frequency that you can easily increase if the snare doesn't have enough body, needs to sound fuller, or needs more muscle.

3 kHz is the range that gives a snare clarity and punch.

6-10 kHz brilliance range

 

Bass:

80-200 Hz adds some fullness, robust low end.

200-300 Hz Mostly lowering for guitars

500-1000 Hz If you need more low-midrange and punch, boost this range.

2.5 - 5 kHz A boost in this area gives your bass guitar more power

 

 

If you finished your equalizer settings and applied them you can now search for “Bass and Treble” in the effects tab. This is simple. Change the Bass, Volume and Treble how you like it. Her can Youlean (The software I mentioned before) help you. You can see if it’s too loud and will mark the high points.

You can always undo your last change with Ctrl+Z.

Mostly i am fine with the results afterwards and save my song. I have to learn how to use all the different filters but this should be enough for the moment. I hope I could help you all to improve and provided some helpful informations.

# **Beginner’s Guide to Free Mastering for Suno + Remix Contest**

## **Introduction**
Mastering music can be overwhelming, especially when you're just starting. Many guides focus on expensive software or advanced techniques that can be hard to grasp for beginners. This tutorial aims to simplify the process, using **only free tools** to help you enhance your Suno tracks quickly and effectively.

At the end, you'll also find details about a **Remix Contest** where you can test your skills and get community feedback. 

---

## **1. Quick AI Mastering with BandLab**
For a **simple one-click mastering solution**, BandLab offers a free mastering tool that can enhance your track with minimal effort.

### **Steps to Master Using BandLab**
1. **Go to BandLab Mastering**: [https://www.bandlab.com/mastering](https://www.bandlab.com/mastering)
2. **Sign up for a free account**.
3. **Upload your Suno track**.
4. Choose from **three free mastering styles**:
   - **Universal**: Balanced sound.
   - **Fire**: Adds warmth and depth.
   - **Clarity**: Enhances details and high frequencies.
5. **Download the mastered track**.

**⚠️ Important:** Avoid using BandLab Mastering if your song has **crackling noises**, as it will amplify them.

---

## **2. Advanced Mastering with Tenacity**
For more control over your mastering, **Tenacity** (a free alternative to Audacity) allows you to fine-tune your track.

### **Download & Install Tenacity**
1. **Download Tenacity 1.3.4**:  
   👉 [https://tenacityaudio.org/](https://tenacityaudio.org/)  
   👉 Direct Download: [Tenacity 1.3.4 (Windows)](https://codeberg.org/tenacityteam/tenacity/releases/download/v1.3.4/tenacity-win-1.3.4-x64.exe)  
2. Install and **open Tenacity**.
3. Go to **Settings** and set your **audio output device**.
4. **Import your track** by dragging it into the program.

---

## **3. Using Youlean Loudness Meter for Volume Control**
Youlean Loudness Meter helps ensure your track meets loudness standards, especially for platforms like YouTube and Spotify.

### **Steps to Measure Your Track's Loudness**
1. **Download Youlean Loudness Meter (Free)**:  
   👉 [https://youlean.co/youlean-loudness-meter/](https://youlean.co/youlean-loudness-meter/)  
2. Open the software and **select your audio driver type** (e.g., **WASAPI System Audio**).
3. Play your track and measure the **LUFS (Loudness Unit Full Scale)**:
   - **Optimal range**: **-14 LUFS to -10 LUFS**.
   - YouTube automatically normalizes volume to **-14 LUFS**.

---

## **4. Equalizing Your Track in Tenacity**
Equalization (EQ) allows you to enhance or reduce specific frequency ranges for a balanced mix.

### **How to Apply EQ in Tenacity**
1. **Select Your Entire Track** → Press **Ctrl+A**.
2. Go to **Effects** → **Filter Curve Equalizer**.
3. Adjust frequencies according to the **instrumental/vocal ranges**:

| Frequency | Range | Usage |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Sub-bass** (<60Hz) | Deep bass | Boost for depth |
| **Bass** (60-150Hz) | Low-end | Adds warmth |
| **Lower Midrange** (150-800Hz) | Body of sound | Boost for fuller sound |
| **Mids** (800Hz-1.6kHz) | Core frequencies | Adjust for clarity |
| **Upper Mids** (1.6-3kHz) | Punch & presence | Boost for energy |
| **Treble** (3kHz-14kHz) | Brightness | Controls sharpness |
| **Super Highs** (14kHz+) | Airy quality | Enhances clarity |

---

## **5. Instrument-Specific EQ Adjustments**
Fine-tune instruments to enhance their sound:

### **Vocal Optimization**
- **Female vocals**: Boost around **250 Hz**.
- **Male vocals**: Boost around **350 Hz**.
- Enhance clarity by raising **3-6 kHz**.

### **Drum Enhancement**
- **Kick drum**: Boost **50-100Hz** for deep bass.
- **Snare drum**:
  - **150Hz**: Adds power.
  - **500Hz**: Makes snare sound fuller.
  - **3kHz**: Enhances clarity.

### **Bass Guitar**
- **80-200Hz**: Adds fullness.
- **500Hz-1kHz**: Increases punch.
- **2.5kHz-5kHz**: Boosts presence.

---

## **6. Adjusting Bass & Treble**
1. Open **Effects** → **Bass and Treble**.
2. Increase or decrease bass & treble as needed.
3. Use **Youlean Meter** to check for loudness spikes.

### **Undo Changes If Needed**
- **Ctrl+Z** to undo any unwanted changes.
- Always **save a backup** before applying permanent edits.

---

## **7. Recommended Equipment for Mastering**
### **Headphones for Clear Sound**
- **AKG K92 Studio Headphones** (or similar).
- Avoid using **gaming headsets**, as they may distort sound.

### **Amplifier for Best Audio Output**
- **Behringer U-Phoria UM2** (can also power studio microphones for sampling).

# **Beginner's Guide to AI Mastering (Free Tools Only) + Remix Contest**

If you're new to music production and struggling to find beginner-friendly, **free** tools for mastering, this guide will help you get started. I'll walk you through a simple AI mastering process, introduce free software for more detailed mastering, and share tips to improve your tracks. At the end, you’ll also find a **Remix Contest** to put your skills to the test.

---

## **1. Quick AI Mastering with BandLab**

### **What is BandLab?**
BandLab offers a **one-click** AI mastering tool that optimizes your track instantly. It's simple, quick, and free.

**👉 Try it here:**  
🔗 [BandLab Mastering](https://www.bandlab.com/mastering)

### **Steps to Use BandLab Mastering:**
1. **Sign up** for a free BandLab account.
2. **Upload your track** (your Suno-generated song).
3. **Choose from three free mastering options:**
   - **Universal** – Balanced sound.
   - **Fire** – Adds warmth and loudness.
   - **Clarity** – Focuses on clarity and brightness.
4. **Download your mastered song** when you're happy with the results.

### **Important Note:**
- If your Suno track has crackling noises, **avoid** using BandLab. It will amplify them and make the song sound worse.

---

## **2. Advanced Free Mastering with Tenacity**

For more control, **Tenacity** is a free, open-source audio editor. It allows you to fine-tune your track with equalizers and effects.

**👉 Download Tenacity:**  
🔗 [Tenacity 1.3.4 (Windows)](https://codeberg.org/tenacityteam/tenacity/releases/download/v1.3.4/tenacity-win-1.3.4-x64.exe)  
🔗 [Official Website](https://tenacityaudio.org/)

### **How to Master Your Track in Tenacity:**
1. **Download & install** Tenacity.
2. **Open Tenacity** and go to **Settings** to select your output device (so you can hear the audio).
3. **Drag and drop your song** into the program.
4. **Select the entire track** using **Ctrl + A**.
5. **Apply an Equalizer**:  
   - Go to **Effects → Equalization → Filter Curve EQ**.
   - Adjust frequencies based on the **frequency guide** below.

---

## **3. Frequency Guide for Mastering**
When adjusting the equalizer, use these frequency ranges to improve your mix:

### **General Frequency Ranges:**
- **Sub-bass** (Below 60 Hz) – Deep bass sounds.
- **Bass** (60-150 Hz) – Adds weight to the mix.
- **Low Midrange** (150-800 Hz) – Affects warmth and body.
- **Midrange** (1 kHz) – Defines clarity and presence.
- **Upper Midrange** (1.6-3 kHz) – Affects attack and definition.
- **Treble** (3 kHz+) – Adjusts brightness and clarity.
- **Airy Highs** (14 kHz+) – Adds sparkle.

### **Vocal Enhancement:**
- **Female vocals:** Boost around **250 Hz**.
- **Male vocals:** Boost around **350 Hz**.
- **Increase clarity:** Boost **3-6 kHz**.

### **Drum Mixing Tips:**
- **Kick Drum:**  
  - 50-100 Hz – Adds deep bass.  
  - 150-250 Hz – Adds mid presence.  
  - 2-4 kHz – Increases punch.

- **Snare Drum:**  
  - 150 Hz – Adds power.  
  - 500 Hz – Makes it fuller.  
  - 3 kHz – Adds clarity.  
  - 6-10 kHz – Enhances brilliance.

- **Bass Guitar:**  
  - 80-200 Hz – Adds warmth.  
  - 500-1000 Hz – Enhances punch.  
  - 2.5-5 kHz – Adds presence.

---

## **4. Measuring Loudness with Youlean Loudness Meter**
To ensure your track isn’t too loud or too quiet, use **Youlean Loudness Meter 2**.

**👉 Download Youlean Loudness Meter:**  
🔗 [Youlean Loudness Meter Free Version](https://youlean.co/youlean-loudness-meter/)

### **How to Use Youlean:**
1. Install and open the software.
2. Choose **System Audio WASAPI** as the driver type.
3. Select your **default output device**.
4. Play your track and measure the **LUFS** (Loudness Units Full Scale).
   - **Ideal range:** **-14 to -10 LUFS**  
   - YouTube adjusts all audio to **-14 LUFS**, so aim for this level.

---

## **5. Recommended Budget Gear**
If you're serious about music production, consider getting **studio headphones** and an **audio interface** for better sound clarity.

### **My Setup:**
🎧 **Headphones:** AKG K92 (or cheaper models)  
🎛 **Audio Interface:** Behringer U-Phoria UM2  
🎤 **Future Setup:** Planning to add a studio microphone for recording samples in Suno.

---

Once you’ve applied EQ settings:
1. Search for **“Bass and Treble”** in the Effects tab.
2. Adjust bass, volume, and treble as needed.
3. Use **Youlean Meter** to check loudness.
4. **Undo any mistakes** with **Ctrl + Z**.
5. Save and export your finished track.

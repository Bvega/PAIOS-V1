USER:
Explain perception  vs perspective in a very simple way

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
**Perception** is how you **see or understand something** based on your senses and experience.  
**Perspective** is the **point of view or angle** you’re looking from—mental, emotional, or physical.

---

### Simple example:

- **Perception:** You see a dog and think, “That’s a scary animal.”  
- **Perspective:** You were bitten by a dog as a child, so you view dogs from a cautious **perspective**.

---

### Another way:

- **Perception = What you see.**  
- **Perspective = Where you’re seeing it from.**

They affect each other: your **perspective shapes your perception.**

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

USER:
How do I get people’s perspectives

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
To get people's **perspectives**, do this:

---

### 1. **Ask open-ended questions**
- Instead of “Do you like it?” ask:  
  → “What do you think about it?”  
  → “How did you see that situation?”  
  → “What led you to feel that way?”

---

### 2. **Listen without interrupting**
- Let them **explain fully** before you respond.  
- Pay attention to **tone, emotion, and word choice**.

---

### 3. **Be curious, not judgmental**
- Even if you disagree, ask:  
  → “What made you think that?”  
  → “Can you tell me more?”

---

### 4. **Put yourself in their shoes**
- Try to imagine:  
  → “What experiences might they have had?”  
  → “What’s important to them?”

---

### 5. **Respect their background**
- Culture, age, gender, history—all influence perspective.  
- Ask with humility: “What’s your take, given your experience?”

---

### 6. **Reflect back what you hear**
- Say: “So you’re saying that you felt ___ because ___?”  
- This shows you’re trying to **understand, not just hear.**

---

Getting perspectives is about **deep listening and real curiosity.**

ASSISTANT:
Brewing a 5-gallon batch of IPA (India Pale Ale) is a fantastic choice! IPAs are known for their hoppy aroma and bitterness, and they come in a variety of styles. For this recipe, I'll guide you through creating a classic American IPA, which balances hop bitterness with a strong malt backbone. Here's a basic overview of the process:

### Step-by-Step IPA Brewing Process

#### 1. Gather Ingredients

**Grains (Malts):**
- 10 lbs (4.5 kg) 2-Row Pale Malt (Base malt)
- 1 lb (0.45 kg) Munich Malt (Adds a malty depth)
- 0.5 lb (0.23 kg) Caramel/Crystal 40L (Adds sweetness and color)

**Hops:**
- 1 oz (28 g) Centennial (Bittering - 60 minutes)
- 1 oz (28 g) Cascade (Flavor - 15 minutes)
- 1 oz (28 g) Citra (Flavor - 10 minutes)
- 1 oz (28 g) Simcoe (Aroma - 5 minutes)
- 1 oz (28 g) Citra (Dry hop - added during fermentation)

**Yeast:**
- 1 packet of American Ale Yeast (e.g., Wyeast 1056 or Safale US-05)

**Other:**
- 1 Whirlfloc tablet (for clarity, optional)
- Corn sugar for priming (if bottling)

#### 2. Equipment Needed

- Brew kettle (at least 8 gallons)
- Mash tun (cooler with a false bottom or a kettle)
- Fermenter (5-gallon glass carboy or plastic bucket)
- Airlock and bung
- Sanitizer (e.g., Star San)
- Hydrometer or refractometer
- Wort chiller (immersion, plate, or counterflow chiller)
- Auto-siphon, tubing, and bottling bucket (if bottling)
- Bottles and caps or a kegging setup

#### 3. Brewing Process

**A. Mashing (Convert Starch to Sugar)**
1. Heat 3.5 gallons of water to 165°F (74°C).
2. Add the crushed grains to the mash tun, aiming for a mash temperature of 152°F (67°C). Hold this temperature for 60 minutes. Stir well to avoid hot spots.
3. After 60 minutes, sparge (rinse) the grains with 170°F (77°C) water until you collect around 6.5 gallons of wort in your kettle.

**B. Boiling (Extract Hop Bitterness and Sterilize Wort)**
1. Bring the wort to a rolling boil. Once boiling, add the hops according to the schedule below:
   - **60 minutes**: Add 1 oz Centennial (Bittering)
   - **15 minutes**: Add 1 oz Cascade (Flavor) and 1 Whirlfloc tablet (if using)
   - **10 minutes**: Add 1 oz Citra (Flavor)
   - **5 minutes**: Add 1 oz Simcoe (Aroma)
2. After the boil (60 minutes total), turn off the heat.

**C. Cooling and Transferring (Prevent Infection and Prepare for Fermentation)**
1. Cool the wort as quickly as possible to 70°F (21°C) using a wort chiller.
2. Transfer the cooled wort to a sanitized fermenter, leaving most of the hop and trub sediment behind.
3. Aerate the wort by shaking or stirring vigorously for a few minutes.

**D. Fermentation (Convert Sugar to Alcohol)**
1. Pitch the yeast into the fermenter and seal with an airlock.
2. Ferment at around 68°F (20°C) for 1-2 weeks or until fermentation is complete. You can check with a hydrometer to ensure final gravity is stable.
3. After the primary fermentation is complete (about a week), add 1 oz of Citra hops directly to the fermenter for dry hopping. Leave it for another 5-7 days.

**E. Packaging (Bottle or Keg)**
1. If bottling, dissolve 4 oz (113 g) of corn sugar in 2 cups of boiling water and add it to a bottling bucket. Siphon the beer into the bucket, mix gently, and bottle.
2. If kegging, transfer the beer to a sanitized keg and carbonate to your desired level.

**F. Conditioning**
- Bottle-conditioned beers should carbonate for 2-3 weeks at room temperature.
- Kegged beers can be force carbonated and ready in a few days.

#### 4. Enjoy!

After conditioning, your IPA should be ready to enjoy! You’ll have a delicious 5-gallon batch of hoppy, aromatic American IPA.

What were the main causes of the First Winter of AI?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ASSISTANT:
The **First AI Winter** (roughly **mid-1970s to early 1980s**) was a period when enthusiasm, funding, and research progress in artificial intelligence declined sharply. Its causes were mainly a mix of **technical limitations**, **unrealistic expectations**, and **economic/political factors**:

---

### 1. **Overpromised Results vs. Underwhelming Performance**
- Early AI researchers in the 1950s–60s made bold claims that human-level reasoning would be achieved soon.
- In practice, programs that worked on toy problems (like playing checkers or solving algebra) failed miserably on real-world tasks.
- Funders and policymakers became disillusioned when progress slowed and expectations were not met.

---

### 2. **Hardware and Computational Limitations**
- Computers in the 1960s–70s lacked the memory and processing power required for advanced reasoning or large-scale learning.
- Symbolic AI systems (based on logic rules and search trees) quickly hit combinatorial explosions — problems became intractable as data grew.

---

### 3. **Critical Reports**
Two highly influential government-commissioned reports directly triggered funding cuts:
- **Lighthill Report (1973, UK):** Criticized AI research for lacking practical results outside narrow domains, leading to severe budget cuts from the British Science Research Council.
- **DARPA (U.S.) Evaluations (mid-1970s):** The U.S. Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency withdrew funding after AI projects like speech recognition (SUR) and machine translation failed to meet deliverables.

---

### 4. **Weakness of Symbolic AI**
- Symbolic AI (or “Good Old-Fashioned AI”) relied on hand-coded rules and logic.
- Systems like SHRDLU and ELIZA demonstrated clever behavior in limited settings but couldn’t generalize or handle ambiguity.
- Researchers realized the brittleness of these approaches when scaled to open-ended problems.

---

### 5. **Economic and Political Context**
- The 1970s oil crisis and economic stagflation tightened public research budgets worldwide.
- Governments prioritized immediate economic returns over speculative long-term research such as AI.

---

### 6. **Lack of Data and Learning Algorithms**
- Machine learning as we know it didn’t exist yet; statistical approaches and neural networks were primitive.
- The 1969 book *Perceptrons* by Minsky & Papert mathematically showed the limitations of early neural nets, which discouraged further funding in connectionist models for years.

---

### **Summary**
The **First AI Winter** was caused by:
> Unrealistic promises + weak computational capacity + poor generalization + critical reports + funding withdrawal.

It ended only when **expert systems** emerged in the early 1980s, briefly reviving interest before the **Second AI Winter** later followed.

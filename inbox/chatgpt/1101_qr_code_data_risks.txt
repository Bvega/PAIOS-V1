QR codes themselves do not inherently capture or transmit your personal information when you scan them. However, they can be used in various ways that might lead to the collection of information. Here's how QR codes might be involved in data capture and sharing:

1. **Encoded Data**: A QR code can store URLs, contact information, or other data. When scanned, the QR code reveals this information or directs you to a website or app.

2. **Data Collection on Websites/Apps**: If a QR code directs you to a website or app, it might collect data such as your IP address, location, and browsing behavior. If you voluntarily provide information on the site (e.g., by filling out a form), that data can also be captured.

3. **Contact Information Sharing**: If a QR code is used to share contact information (e.g., a digital business card), it can automatically add that information to your device. This doesn't directly share your information with the QR code creator unless you opt to provide it through additional actions.

4. **Tracking and Analytics**: Some QR codes are used for marketing purposes and may include tracking mechanisms to analyze user engagement, such as how many people scan the code and from which location.

5. **Security Risks**: Malicious QR codes can lead to phishing sites or prompt you to download malware. Always be cautious about what you scan, especially from unknown or suspicious sources.

### How to Protect Your Information

- **Verify Sources**: Only scan QR codes from trusted and verified sources.
- **Check URLs**: When scanning a QR code, verify the URL it leads to before clicking on any links or downloading anything.
- **Use Security Apps**: Consider using mobile security apps that can check the safety of URLs accessed via QR codes.
- **Manage Permissions**: Be mindful of permissions requested by websites or apps accessed via QR codes.

### Conclusion

The QR code itself does not inherently capture information about who you exchange data with, but the websites or apps it directs you to might collect data if you interact with them. If you’re concerned about privacy, it's important to be cautious and aware of where the QR code leads and what information you choose to provide after scanning it.
